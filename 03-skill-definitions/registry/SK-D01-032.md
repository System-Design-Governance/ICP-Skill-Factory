## Description

Collect, analyze, and disseminate threat intelligence relevant to OT/ICS environments per IEC 62443-2-1 (ID24) Tier 3 procedure (§5.3–5.4). This skill operationalizes threat intelligence as a continuous process that gathers external threat information from multiple sources, analyzes relevance to the organization's specific OT/ICS environment and threat landscape, and converts raw intelligence into actionable security recommendations. Threat intelligence informs multiple operational defense activities: SK-D01-018 (Continuous Monitoring) incorporates threat signatures and detection rules; SK-D01-025 (SBOM Analysis) prioritizes vulnerability assessment based on threat intelligence; SK-D01-011 (Compliance Gap Analysis) incorporates threat context into risk prioritization; and SK-D01-016/017 (Incident Response and Investigation) leverage threat intelligence for incident characterization and root cause analysis. This skill is mandatory for Tier 3 organizations and establishes the intelligence capability required to defend OT/ICS systems against known and emerging threats.

## Description — Objectives

- Establish and maintain threat intelligence collection infrastructure for OT/ICS environments
- Subscribe to and integrate multiple threat intelligence sources (OSINT, ICS-CERT, vendor bulletins, sector-specific feeds, dark web sources)
- Analyze raw threat intelligence for relevance to organization's specific OT/ICS environment, criticality ranking, and recommended actions
- Maintain threat intelligence database with vulnerabilities, threat actors, indicators of compromise (IOCs), and attack patterns relevant to OT/ICS
- Produce actionable intelligence reports informing detection rule development (SK-D01-018), vulnerability assessment priorities (SK-D01-025), and incident investigation (SK-D01-017)
- Track threat actor tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) targeting operational technology systems
- Support incident investigation with threat intelligence context and indicator enrichment
- Disseminate intelligence to stakeholders (SOC, engineering teams, management) in appropriate formats and timelines

## Description — Scope

**In Scope:** Threat intelligence source identification and subscription; open-source intelligence (OSINT) collection and analysis; ICS-CERT advisory processing; vendor security bulletin monitoring; sector-specific threat feed integration; vulnerability intelligence collection and analysis; threat actor profiling; indicators of compromise (IOCs) collection and enrichment; intelligence gap analysis; dissemination and collaboration with SOC and engineering teams.

**Out of Scope:** Development of classified intelligence sources or foreign intelligence operations (outside scope of corporate security); real-time 24/7 security monitoring (handled by SK-D01-018); vulnerability remediation implementation (assigned to engineering); incident response operations (handled by SK-D01-016); strategic threat assessment (governance-level activity).

## Description — Key Concepts

- **Threat Intelligence:** Processed information about threats, vulnerabilities, threat actors, and attack patterns that enables informed security decision-making
- **Threat Intelligence Lifecycle:** Phases of planning, collection, analysis, dissemination, and feedback
- **Open-Source Intelligence (OSINT):** Intelligence collected from publicly available sources (websites, forums, academic papers, government publications, media)
- **ICS-CERT Advisory:** Vulnerability notification issued by CISA (Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Security Agency) specifically for industrial control systems
- **Indicators of Compromise (IOCs):** Technical artifacts indicative of a security breach (malware hashes, IP addresses, domain names, file paths, registry keys)
- **Threat Actor:** Individual or group conducting cyber attacks; characterized by motivation, capability, TTPs, and target profiles
- **Tactics, Techniques, and Procedures (TTPs):** How threat actors conduct attacks (motivation and goals = tactics; specific methods = techniques; attack execution steps = procedures)
- **Vulnerability Intelligence:** Information about software/hardware vulnerabilities including severity, affected systems, availability of exploits, and patch availability

## Description — Methodology

Threat intelligence collection and analysis for OT/ICS follows a structured intelligence lifecycle:

**Phase 1: Threat Intelligence Program Establishment**
- Define intelligence requirements based on organizational threat model (from SK-D01-035):
  - What threat actors are targeting our sector/industry?
  - What OT/ICS platforms and products do we use that are targeted?
  - What vulnerabilities are likely to be exploited?
  - What attack patterns are most relevant to our operational environment?
- Identify intelligence source priorities:
  - Primary sources: ICS-CERT/CISA, vendor security bulletins for deployed products, sector-specific threat feeds
  - Secondary sources: General cybersecurity threat intelligence, research papers, academic sources
  - Tertiary sources: Dark web monitoring (if organizational capability exists), foreign intelligence (if available)
- Establish intelligence collection infrastructure:
  - Threat intelligence platform (if budget available) or manual collection/analysis
  - Automated subscription to ICS-CERT mailing list, vendor security bulletins
  - Alert and notification setup for new vulnerabilities affecting deployed systems
  - Secure database for threat intelligence storage and analysis
- Define roles and responsibilities:
  - Threat intelligence analyst (primary collector and analyzer)
  - SOC team (consumer of threat intelligence for detection rule development)
  - Engineering team (consumer for vulnerability assessment prioritization)
  - Management (consumer of executive summary and threat assessment reports)

**Phase 2: Continuous Threat Intelligence Collection**
- Monitor and collect threat intelligence from multiple sources:
  - **ICS-CERT/CISA Advisories:** Review weekly advisories for vulnerabilities, incident trends, and threat activity; filter for relevance to deployed systems
  - **Vendor Security Bulletins:** Subscribe to security bulletin mailing lists for all deployed OT/ICS vendors; prioritize CVEs affecting organization's deployed products
  - **Sector-Specific Threat Feeds:** Subscribe to threat feeds relevant to organization's industry (utilities, manufacturing, pharmaceutical, etc.); monitor for threats targeting sector-specific systems and TTPs
  - **Public Threat Intelligence:** Monitor threat intelligence sharing communities (VirusTotal, AlienVault OTX) for emerging malware, IOCs, and attack patterns
  - **Academic and Research Sources:** Review published security research for novel vulnerabilities, attack techniques, and threat actor methodologies
  - **News and Media Monitoring:** Track technology media, security blogs, and industry publications for emerging threats, major incidents, and threat actor activity
- Establish collection schedule and update frequency:
  - Daily: ICS-CERT/CISA advisories, critical vendor security bulletins, SOC alert feeds
  - Weekly: Sector-specific threat intelligence, threat intelligence community feeds, research publications
  - Monthly: Strategic threat landscape assessment, threat actor profiling updates
- Maintain threat intelligence source inventory with subscription status, update frequency, and cost (if applicable)

**Phase 3: Threat Intelligence Analysis and Contextualization**
- For each collected threat intelligence item, analyze relevance and impact:
  - **CVE/Vulnerability Analysis:**
    - Identify all deployed products affected by CVE
    - Assess severity using CVSS and organization-specific risk factors (asset criticality, exposure)
    - Determine if exploit is publicly available or in active exploitation
    - Evaluate patch availability and timeline for remediation
    - Assign remediation priority (Critical, High, Medium, Low) based on criticality and exploitability
  - **Threat Actor Profiling:**
    - Document threat actor name/alias, known aliases, and historical activity
    - Assess motivation (financial gain, espionage, disruption, activism)
    - Identify target sectors and typical attack patterns
    - Map to MITRE ATT&CK or other TTP frameworks
    - Assess capability level (inexperienced, intermediate, advanced, nation-state)
  - **Attack Pattern Analysis:**
    - Identify common attack sequences and techniques used by threat actors
    - Assess likelihood of attack vector affecting organization
    - Determine detection opportunities (which TTPs can be detected by monitoring)
    - Identify mitigation/prevention opportunities (which controls reduce attack likelihood)
  - **IOC Enrichment:**
    - Collect indicators of compromise (malware hashes, C2 domains, malicious IPs)
    - Enrich IOCs with context (first seen date, attribution, campaign, associated malware)
    - Feed IOCs to SK-D01-018 (Continuous Monitoring) for detection rule integration
- Document all analysis with sources and confidence levels:
  - Confidence: High (from reputable source with full details), Medium (partial information, reasonable inference), Low (limited information, high uncertainty)
  - Sources: Cite specific advisory, bulletin, research paper, or community feed

**Phase 4: Intelligence Database Maintenance**
- Maintain threat intelligence database with:
  - **Vulnerability Registry:** CVE ID, affected products, severity, patch status, remediation priority, organizational impact assessment
  - **Threat Actor Registry:** Actor name/aliases, motivation, capability level, target profile, known TTPs, associated campaigns
  - **IOC Database:** Malware hashes, C2 domains, malicious IPs, with context (campaign, malware family, attribution, detection methods)
  - **Attack Pattern Registry:** Common TTP sequences, detection opportunities, mitigation recommendations
- Update database as new intelligence is collected and analyzed
- Perform quarterly database review to archive stale information and identify gaps
- Maintain database security with access controls, encryption, and audit logging

**Phase 5: Intelligence Dissemination and Stakeholder Communication**
- Produce intelligence reports for different stakeholder audiences:
  - **Monthly Threat Intelligence Report:** For SOC and engineering teams; detailed threat analysis, vulnerability assessment, IOC feeds, detection rule recommendations
  - **Quarterly Strategic Threat Assessment:** For management/governance; threat landscape summary, threat actor activity trends, organizational risk assessment, resource recommendations
  - **Urgent Intelligence Alerts:** For critical vulnerabilities, zero-days, active exploitation affecting deployed systems; distributed within 24 hours to all stakeholders
  - **Vulnerability Assessment Briefing:** For engineering team; prioritized list of vulnerabilities requiring remediation based on criticality, exploitability, and threat intelligence context
- Distribute IOCs to SK-D01-018 (Continuous Monitoring) for incorporation into detection rules
- Provide intelligence context to SK-D01-017 (Incident Investigation) for incident characterization and attribution
- Collaborate with SOC to incorporate threat intelligence into alert tuning and baseline adjustments
- Establish feedback loop from stakeholders (SOC, engineering) for intelligence gap identification and prioritization

**Phase 6: Intelligence Gap Analysis and Program Improvement**
- Quarterly review of threat intelligence program:
  - Assess coverage of organizational threat model (are we collecting intelligence on relevant threats?)
  - Identify gaps (threat actors, vulnerabilities, attack patterns, sectors we are not monitoring)
  - Evaluate source quality and relevance (are sources providing actionable intelligence?)
  - Assess stakeholder satisfaction (are reports meeting customer needs? Are recommendations being acted upon?)
- Recommend intelligence collection improvements:
  - New threat intelligence sources or feeds to subscribe to
  - Intelligence analysis enhancement (deeper technical analysis, threat actor profiling, attribution)
  - Intelligence dissemination improvements (different report formats, different audiences)
  - Integration with additional systems (SOAR, threat intelligence platform, threat modeling tools)
- Update threat intelligence requirements annually based on changes to threat landscape and organizational risk profile

## Description — Practical Guidance

- **Pitfall:** Collecting threat intelligence without analysis, leading to information overload. **Guidance:** Filter threat intelligence by organizational relevance; analyze each vulnerability for organizational impact; prioritize threats by likelihood and criticality; focus on threat actors and attack patterns that target your sector and systems.
- **Pitfall:** Threat intelligence is collected but not disseminated to stakeholders. **Guidance:** Establish regular reporting cadence (monthly for SOC/engineering, quarterly for management); distribute IOCs to detection team; create feedback loop for intelligence gap identification.
- **Pitfall:** Intelligence is not actionable (too high-level, missing implementation details). **Guidance:** Convert threat intelligence to specific detection rules (for SOC), vulnerability remediation priorities (for engineering), and risk mitigation recommendations; include technical details and IOCs that can be acted upon.
- **Pitfall:** Threat intelligence sources are not continuously monitored, missing new threats. **Guidance:** Establish collection schedule and automate where possible (RSS feeds, mailing lists); perform daily review of critical sources; set up alerts for vulnerabilities affecting deployed systems.
- **Pitfall:** Intelligence analysis lacks confidence levels, making prioritization difficult. **Guidance:** Rate confidence of each analysis item (High/Medium/Low based on source quality and completeness); distinguish between facts (observed) and assessments (inferred); cite sources for all analysis.
- **Pitfall:** Threat intelligence program becomes stale or loses organizational support. **Guidance:** Conduct quarterly program review; demonstrate impact by showing how intelligence informed detection improvements or vulnerability remediation; solicit stakeholder feedback; update threat intelligence requirements annually based on risk profile changes.

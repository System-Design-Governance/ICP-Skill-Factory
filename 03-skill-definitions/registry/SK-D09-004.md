## Description

This skill encompasses the systematic management of all project document deliverables per the contract and ID03 requirements, tracking each deliverable through its lifecycle from draft authoring through review, approval, and final handover to the customer. The Document Delivery Checklist (DDC) is a master control document that itemizes all contractual deliverables, their scheduled delivery dates, responsible authors, review schedules, and delivery status. This skill ensures contractual compliance, prevents missed deliverables, and provides transparency to project governance and the customer. The scope includes establishing the DDC at project initiation, maintaining it throughout design phases, and verifying completion at project gate reviews (particularly Gate 3, the Design Review Gate).

## Inputs

- Contract and Statement of Work (SOW) specifying deliverable requirements and delivery schedule
- Project schedule and milestone dates (from SK-D11-001: Project Planning)
- Design plan and work breakdown structure (WBS) identifying which skill/discipline is responsible for each deliverable
- Technical specifications for deliverable format, quality standards, and review criteria (per ID03 §5.5.3)
- Gate 3 (Design Review) checklist template and review criteria
- Previous deliverable checklists from similar projects (for template adaptation)

## Outputs

- Document Delivery Checklist (DDC): master spreadsheet/document listing all contractual deliverables with status (Draft, Under Review, Approved, Delivered), responsible owner, planned delivery date, and actual completion date
- Deliverable Status Report: monthly or gate-level report summarizing completeness percentage, at-risk deliverables, and mitigation actions
- Gate 3 Delivery Verification Checklist: formal sign-off confirming all design-phase deliverables meet quality and completeness criteria
- Deliverable Handover Record: signed acknowledgment from customer upon final delivery of all project documents
- Lessons Learned on Deliverable Management: process improvements for tracking and delivery efficiency

## IEC 62443 Lifecycle Stages

- R0 (Concept) — DDC is initiated with project planning and identifies documentation requirements
- R2 (Detailed Design) — DDC tracks design documentation deliverables through review cycles
- R3 (Implementation) — DDC ensures implementation documentation is delivered on schedule
- R4 (Commissioning) — DDC tracks commissioning and operational documentation deliverables
- R5 (Operations) — DDC supports ongoing documentation and compliance record maintenance

## Roles

- DES (Design Engineer) or PM (Project Manager) — primary: establishes and maintains DDC, coordinates deliverable statuses
- SYS (System Architect) — provides design deliverable milestones and status updates
- QAM (Quality Assurance Manager) — verifies deliverables meet quality and completeness standards
- GAM (Gate Approval Manager) — reviews DDC at gate reviews and verifies contractual compliance
- Customer Representative — approves deliverables and provides sign-off on final handover

## Acceptance Criteria

1. DDC includes 100% of deliverables specified in the contract and SOW, organized by project phase and delivery date
2. Each deliverable has a clearly assigned owner, planned delivery date, and documented review/approval criteria
3. DDC status is updated at least monthly and reported at all project gate reviews with current percentage complete
4. All at-risk deliverables (past due or approaching deadline) have documented mitigation actions and re-planning
5. Gate 3 sign-off confirms that all design-phase deliverables are complete, approved, and ready for implementation phase
6. Final project closeout includes a Deliverable Handover Record signed by both project team and customer, confirming receipt and acceptance of all deliverables

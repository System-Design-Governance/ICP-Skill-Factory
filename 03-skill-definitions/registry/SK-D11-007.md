## Description

This skill encompasses the design and execution of a comprehensive Stakeholder Communication Plan that identifies all project stakeholders, defines their communication needs and preferences, establishes communication schedules and channels, and ensures consistent, timely, and appropriate information flow throughout all project phases. Stakeholders include customer representatives, regulatory bodies, project team members, executives, operations personnel, security personnel, and end-users. The communication plan aligns with project governance (ID01 §6.0) and ensures that stakeholders have the information they need to make decisions, approve deliverables, resolve issues, and support project success. The scope spans project initiation through operations handover, with communication tailored to each phase and stakeholder group.

## Inputs

- Project Charter and Statement of Work (SOW) — defines stakeholder expectations and communication contractual requirements
- Stakeholder Analysis and Register (from SK-D11-001: Project Planning) — identifies stakeholders, their roles, interests, and influence levels
- Project Schedule and Milestones (from SK-D11-001: Project Planning) — drives communication timing and frequency
- Project Risk Register (from SK-D11-017: Risk Management Plan) — high-risk items may require targeted stakeholder communication
- Organizational communication policies and escalation procedures
- Prior project communication plans and lessons learned (for template and best practices)
- Regulatory and compliance requirements (if applicable)

## Outputs

- Stakeholder Communication Plan: document specifying stakeholder groups, communication objectives for each group, communication content and frequency, responsible parties, distribution methods, and success metrics
- Stakeholder Register: structured list of stakeholders with contact information, role, communication preferences, and frequency
- Communication Schedule: calendar of recurring communications (status reports, reviews, approval gates) aligned with project milestones
- Communication Templates: email templates, status report formats, presentation decks for consistency and efficiency
- Escalation Procedures: decision authority levels and escalation paths for different issue categories
- Communication Effectiveness Metrics: methods for measuring stakeholder satisfaction, information timeliness, and engagement

## IEC 62443 Lifecycle Stages

- R0 (Concept) — communication plan is established at project initiation to align stakeholder expectations
- R1 (Security Requirements) — requirements phase communication ensures stakeholder alignment on security objectives
- R2 (Detailed Design) — design review communications ensure stakeholder approval of major design decisions
- R3 (Implementation) — implementation phase communications track progress and manage stakeholder concerns
- R4 (Commissioning) — commissioning communications prepare stakeholders for system handover and operations
- R5 (Operations) — post-handover communications support operational readiness and continuous improvement

## Roles

- PM (Project Manager) or GAM (Gate Approval Manager) — primary: establishes and executes communication plan, owns stakeholder engagement
- Communications Specialist (optional) — supports with communication content development and distribution
- Project Team Members (SYS, DES, STC, SAC, QAM) — contribute to phase-specific communications and reviews
- Customer Representative — defines customer communication preferences and approval processes
- Executives/Sponsors — receive executive summaries and approval communications; may sponsor escalation decisions

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Communication Plan identifies all significant stakeholders (customer, regulatory, operations, team, executive) and documents their information needs, communication preferences (email, meetings, reports), and desired frequency
2. Communication Plan defines specific communication objectives for each major project phase (Initiation, Design, Implementation, Commissioning, Handover) and specifies content, schedule, and responsible parties
3. Escalation procedures clearly define decision authority levels (e.g., issues resolved by PM, Design Lead, Executive Sponsor) with timelines and escalation paths
4. Communication Schedule is aligned with project milestones and gate reviews; recurring communications (weekly status meetings, monthly progress reports) are documented with dates, attendees, and distribution method
5. All stakeholders have received and acknowledged the Communication Plan; preferences and contact information are current and verified
6. Communication effectiveness is monitored through periodic stakeholder surveys or feedback; feedback is used to adjust communication methods and frequency as needed

## Description

Prepare and assemble the complete deliverable package required for each ICP design gate review (Gate 0 through Gate 3), verify all blocking conditions are met, and facilitate the gate decision process. Under ICP governance (GOV-SD), this is an execution-level skill that prepares the inputs for the gate review governed by SK-D11-017 ⏳ (Gate Review Governance & Blocking Condition Verification). This skill focuses on the practical work of gathering deliverables, verifying document completeness, organizing review materials, and coordinating stakeholder participation. The boundary of this skill is gate preparation logistics and deliverable assembly; the governance decision authority, blocking condition enforcement, and formal approval/rejection reside in SK-D11-017 ⏳.

## Inputs

- Gate-specific deliverable checklist (from GOV-SD per gate):
  - **Gate 0**: requirement clarification record, risk pre-disclosure list, feasibility assessment, CBOM (commercially usable / design non-binding), concept architecture (conceptual / non-binding)
  - **Gate 1**: design baseline documents, integrated risk assessment (Lite acceptable), SR checklist, traceability matrix, document register, file clearance list
  - **Gate 2**: design change request, impact analysis, SL Decision Record update (if applicable)
  - **Gate 3**: final design package (versioned/dated/owner-labeled), Complete risk assessment, SR checklist (all Implemented or N/A), residual risk register (Appendix C), risk traceability verification, handover meeting agenda
- Current design artifacts from all contributing skill outputs
- Document register with version tracking (from SK-D09-004 ⏳: Document Delivery Checklist Management)
- Prior gate review records and action items (for Gates 1–3)

## Outputs

- Gate Review Package: organized collection of all required deliverables per the applicable gate checklist
- Deliverable Completeness Matrix: checklist showing each required document, its current version, owner, approval status, and readiness assessment (Ready / Partial / Missing)
- Pre-Review Gap Report: identified gaps or incomplete items with remediation plan and timeline
- Review Meeting Agenda and Logistics: date, participants (per RACI), materials distribution, decision points
- Post-Review Action Item Register: documented decisions, action items with owners and deadlines, conditions for re-review if gate not passed
- **Gate 3 specific**: 12-item design delivery checklist (per GOV-SD) with pass/fail status for each item

## Roles

- System Design Governance Lead (GOV-SDP) — Gate 1 and Gate 3 approver; leads review sessions
- Head of System Design (GOV-SDP) — Gate 0 and Gate 2 (major change) approver; final arbiter for exceptions
- SAC (Security Architect) — prepares security-related deliverables, presents risk assessment findings
- Security Engineering Role (GOV-SDP) — prepares integrated risk assessment outputs, SR verification evidence
- Design QA Role (GOV-SDP) — verifies document completeness, version consistency, traceability coverage
- Pre-Gate Design Support (GOV-SDP) — prepares Gate 0 input package (5 required documents)
- Risk Acceptance Authority — separate signer at Gate 3 (not the same as design approver)

## Dependencies

### Hard Dependencies (Required Before Execution)

- Gate-specific deliverables must be produced by their respective skill owners (varies per gate)
- SK-D09-004 ⏳: Document Delivery Checklist Management — document register must be current to assess completeness
- GOV-SD gate checklist definitions — defines what constitutes a complete gate package

### Soft Dependencies (Enhances Effectiveness)

- SK-D11-017 ⏳: Gate Review Governance & Blocking Condition Verification — governance-level blocking condition definitions inform preparation priorities
- SK-D11-018 ⏳: Design Quality & Traceability Verification — Design QA pre-verification reduces gate review failures
- SK-D01-036 ⏳: Risk Source Traceability & Residual Risk Register — formal traceability coding needed for Gate 3 20% sampling

## Automation Potential

**Level:** Partial

Deliverable checklist population, version tracking, and completeness gap detection can be automated through document management integration. Meeting scheduling and materials distribution are automatable. However, gap remediation judgment, stakeholder coordination for incomplete items, and review facilitation require human interaction. The 12-item Gate 3 checklist verification requires human assessment for most items.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Deliverable completeness matrix shows 100% "Ready" status for all mandatory gate items before the review is scheduled
2. Pre-review gap report is distributed to stakeholders at least 5 working days before the gate review date
3. All gate-specific blocking conditions have been pre-verified and documented (blocking condition list varies per gate per GOV-SD)
4. Review meeting includes all required RACI participants as defined in GOV-SDP
5. Post-review action item register is distributed within 2 working days of the review session
6. For Gate 3: all 12 items on the design delivery checklist are individually verified and signed off

## Estimated Effort

| Level | Effort | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Junior (< 2 yr) | 5–8 person-days per gate | Assumes ~20 deliverables to track, standard complexity; includes preparation, coordination, and post-review documentation |
| Senior (5+ yr) | 3–5 person-days per gate | Same scope; senior efficiently identifies gaps and coordinates remediation |

Notes: Gate 3 is the most effort-intensive due to the 12-item checklist, residual risk verification, and handover logistics. Gate 2 effort depends on change scope (minor changes may require only 1–2 days). Gate 0 is the lightest but requires Pre-Gate Design Support coordination.

## Description

Manage the preparation, review, execution, and tracking of Non-Disclosure Agreements (NDAs) and confidentiality agreements required before sensitive project information is exchanged between ICP and customers, vendors, or subcontractors. NDA management is typically the first administrative gate in any Pre-Gate 0 engagement — no technical information should be shared without appropriate confidentiality protections in place. This skill covers NDA template selection, scope customization, review coordination, execution tracking, and obligation monitoring; the underlying legal review falls outside engineering scope (legal department responsibility), and the vendor security risk assessment that may trigger NDA requirements falls under SK-D01-024.

## Inputs

- ICP standard NDA template (ID25, Tier 3: MF-AM-07-09 Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement)
- Customer/employer proposed NDA (if customer-initiated)
- Project scope description (from SK-D14-001 or preliminary engagement terms)
- Parties involved: customer, subcontractors, vendors requiring confidentiality coverage
- Information classification guidelines (from SK-D01-033 ⏳ or organizational policy)

## Outputs

- Executed NDA Register: tracking table of all NDAs with: parties, effective date, expiry date, scope of information covered, special terms, status (Draft / Under Review / Executed / Expired)
- NDA Scope Customization Notes: per-agreement documentation of scope adjustments from standard template (if any)
- Obligation Tracking Calendar: NDA expiry alerts, renewal requirements, information return/destruction obligations post-expiry
- NDA Compliance Checklist: verification that all parties involved in information exchange have appropriate NDA coverage before information sharing begins

## Automation Potential

**Level:** Partial

NDA template generation and pre-population can be automated. Expiry tracking and renewal alerts can be fully automated. NDA register maintenance can be automated through contract management platforms. However, NDA scope customization requires judgment (what information needs protection), non-standard clause negotiation requires human interaction, and compliance verification requires contextual assessment.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. NDA register maintained: all active NDAs tracked with parties, dates, scope, and status — no information-sharing engagements without NDA coverage
2. NDA executed before technical information exchange begins — compliance verified via NDA checklist
3. NDA scope covers all categories of sensitive information to be exchanged (technical, commercial, personal data where applicable)
4. Expiry tracking active: all NDAs with expiry dates have renewal alerts set minimum 60 days before expiry
5. Non-standard terms documented: any deviations from ICP standard NDA template (ID25) recorded with legal approval reference
6. Post-project NDA obligations tracked: information return/destruction requirements monitored after project completion

## Estimated Effort

| Level | Effort | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Junior (< 2 yr) | 1–2 person-days | Assumes standard bilateral NDA using ICP template; includes preparation, coordination, and register setup |
| Senior (5+ yr) | 0.5–1 person-days | Same scope; senior handles standard NDAs efficiently |

Notes: Effort increases significantly for complex multi-party NDAs, customer-initiated NDAs requiring negotiation, or international agreements with jurisdiction-specific requirements. Standard bilateral NDAs using ICP template (ID25) are straightforward. NDA management effort per project is front-loaded (initial execution) with minimal ongoing effort (expiry monitoring).

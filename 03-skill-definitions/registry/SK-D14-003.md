## Description

Evaluate the technical feasibility of the proposed OT/ICS cybersecurity solution, assessing whether the customer requirements can be met with available technology, within site constraints, and at acceptable risk levels. Under GOV-SD, the feasibility assessment is one of the 5 mandatory Gate 0 inputs produced by the Pre-Gate Design Support Role, and its output is explicitly advisory (non-binding) — the System Architect at Gate 1 can adjust or reject feasibility conclusions without violating prior commitments. This skill covers the technical evaluation and feasibility judgment; the requirement specification that defines what is being assessed falls under SK-D14-001, the site survey providing constraint inputs falls under SK-D14-011, and the full Pre-Gate 0 input package assembly falls under SK-D14-018 ⏳.

## Inputs

- Requirements specification (from SK-D14-001) — what the solution must achieve
- Tender security requirements (from SK-D14-010) — security-specific requirements and SL-T proposals
- Site survey findings and constraint register (from SK-D14-011) — physical and environmental constraints
- Existing infrastructure inventory (from SK-D14-012) — brownfield baseline and integration challenges
- Preliminary security classification (from SK-D14-014) — proposed SL-T levels and scope implications
- Vendor product capabilities and limitations (from market research and vendor discussions)
- ICP internal capability assessment: available expertise, tool proficiency, prior project experience

## Outputs

- Technical Feasibility Assessment Report:
  - Feasibility verdict per major requirement area: Feasible / Feasible-with-Conditions / Not-Feasible — with justification per area
  - Technical risk identification: risks that could prevent successful delivery, with likelihood and impact assessment
  - Technology selection recommendations: recommended platforms, products, and architectural approaches (advisory, not binding)
  - Constraint impact analysis: how site constraints and existing infrastructure affect solution design
  - Capability gap analysis: ICP capability gaps that require training, hiring, or subcontracting
  - Alternative approaches: where primary approach has significant risk, alternative technical approaches identified
  - Assumptions register: technical assumptions underlying the feasibility verdict (must be validated during R1)
- Risk Pre-Disclosure List: known technical risks surfaced for Gate 0 decision-makers (per GOV-SD requirement)
- Feasibility Summary (one-page): executive-ready summary with overall feasibility verdict and top-3 risks

## Automation Potential

**Level:** Human-Only

Technical feasibility assessment is fundamentally a judgment-intensive activity requiring senior engineering expertise. While data gathering (product comparisons, capability matrices) can be partially systematized, the core feasibility evaluation — assessing whether a complex OT cybersecurity solution can be delivered within constraints — requires deep domain experience, contextual reasoning, and risk judgment that cannot be meaningfully automated.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Feasibility verdict provided for every major requirement area (functional, security, operational, interface) — no requirement areas left unassessed
2. Technical risks identified with likelihood and impact classification — minimum 5 risks for a typical project, each with mitigation strategy or acceptance rationale
3. Risk pre-disclosure list produced and suitable for Gate 0 decision-makers — risks stated in business-understandable language
4. Assumptions register documented — every assumption underlying the feasibility verdict explicitly stated with validation plan for R1
5. Feasibility summary produced — one-page executive document with overall verdict and top-3 risks
6. Report marked as advisory (non-binding) per GOV-SD Pre-Gate 0 boundary — no language implying design commitment

## Estimated Effort

| Level | Effort | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Junior (< 2 yr) | 5–8 person-days | Assumes medium-complexity project; includes analysis, vendor research, report writing, and review |
| Senior (5+ yr) | 3–5 person-days | Same scope; senior leverages prior project patterns and rapid risk identification |

Notes: Feasibility assessment is typically performed by senior engineers due to the judgment requirements. Complex multi-vendor or multi-site projects may require 2× effort. Tight tender timelines may compress the assessment, increasing risk of incomplete analysis. Feasibility assessment quality is a GOV-SDP KPI for the Pre-Gate Design Support Role.

## Description

Calculate and select appropriate wire/cable sizes for both control circuits (low-current signal wiring) and power circuits (high-current power distribution) based on current capacity, voltage drop limits, short-circuit withstand capability, and applicable electrical standards (IEC 60228 for wire insulation and conductor sizing, IEC 60364 for installation practices, NEC/NFPA for North American projects). Wire sizing is a critical engineering task that directly affects system safety, reliability, and economic efficiency. Undersized wires present fire and equipment damage risks; oversized wires increase cost without benefit. This skill requires knowledge of load analysis (from power system design), circuit impedance analysis (for short-circuit calculations), and regulatory compliance with applicable electrical codes. Wire sizing calculations feed into wiring diagrams (SK-D06-002), terminal block schedules (SK-D06-003), fabrication drawings (SK-D06-004), and bill-of-materials planning.

## Inputs

- Load analysis and power flow study (from SK-D03-001 ⏳: Load Flow and Power System Analysis)
- Control system I/O specifications and signal levels (from SK-D04-001 ⏳: Functional Electrical Schematic Design)
- Equipment datasheets (PLC, drives, relay modules, solenoids, heaters, lights, sensors with operating current specs)
- Power system fault study and short-circuit current calculation (from power system engineering studies; e.g., ETAP or PSS/E output)
- Cable routing and conduit information (intended routing path, ambient temperature, conduit fill percentages)
- Voltage drop allowance specifications (customer requirement; typical: 3% for power circuits, 5% for control circuits)
- Regulatory framework and applicable electrical codes (IEC 60228, IEC 60364, NEC, local distribution utility standards)
- Environmental conditions (ambient temperature, altitude, outdoor vs. indoor, corrosive environment affecting insulation selection)

## Outputs

- Wire Sizing Calculation Report (structured table or narrative document):
  - Circuit ID (from schematic, e.g., "Motor_M01_Power", "Sensor_S001_Signal")
  - Circuit description and purpose (e.g., "480V 3-phase power to Motor 1", "4-20mA analog input from pressure transmitter")
  - Load current or signal current (Amperes for power; mA for signal/control)
  - Circuit length (one-way distance from source to load, meters or feet)
  - Voltage drop allowance (% or Volts)
  - Selected wire gauge (AWG, mm², or IEC designation, e.g., "4mm² Cu" per IEC 60228)
  - Insulation type and voltage rating (e.g., "600V XHHW-2", "IEC 60227 0.6/1kV PVC")
  - Justification and calculation method:
    - For power circuits: maximum current (Amperes) from load, applied derating factors (temperature, conduit fill, ambient), wire ampacity from IEC 60228 or NEC Table, voltage drop check: (2 × R × I × L) / 1000 ≤ allowance
    - For control circuits: signal current level, wire capacitance limits (if high-frequency signals), voltage drop constraints
  - Short-circuit withstand check (for high-fault-current zones): calculated short-circuit current (kA) vs. wire withstand capability per IEC 60364-4-43 or NEC Table 250.122
  - Cable type recommendation (single-core, multi-core, shielded, armored, with recommendations for environmental protection)
  - Color code or identification method (per IEC 60445 or NEC)
  - Cost estimate (per meter/foot) for budget planning
- Wire/Cable Schedule (consolidated table): circuit ID, conductor size, insulation type, quantity, cable length, total material cost
- Selection rationale document explaining any design choices (e.g., why a larger wire was selected for reliability; why shielded cable is required; why a specific insulation type is necessary)
- Revision-controlled calculation file per SK-D09-005

## Tools

- IEC 60228 Copper Wire Tables (ampacity vs. gauge and temperature)
- IEC 60364-5-52 Cable and Conduit Selection calculator tools (spreadsheet or specialized software)
- NEC Article 310 / Table 310 (North American ampacity tables) or equivalent per jurisdiction
- ETAP / PowerWorld (power system short-circuit calculation to determine fault current for wire withstand checks)
- Excel / MathCAD (calculation worksheets with embedded formulas for voltage drop and derating)
- Voltage drop calculator tools (web-based or software, e.g., Southwire, Anixter, or Belden cable calculators)

## Standards

- IEC 60228: Conductors of insulated cables — specifies conductor cross-sections (mm²) and ampacities (Amperes) for copper and aluminum conductors
- IEC 60227: Polyvinyl chloride (PVC) insulated cables of rated voltages up to and including 450/750 V — common control circuit insulation
- IEC 60364-5-52: Selection and erection of electrical equipment — cable routing and protective measures
- IEC 60364-4-43: Protection against overcurrent — short-circuit withstand and protection device coordination
- IEC 60445: Identification of conductors by color or numerals — wire color coding standards
- NEC Article 310 (Table 310.15(B)(16)) and Article 250 (for North American projects)
- IEC 61936-1: Power installations exceeding 1 kV AC — high-voltage cable selection (if applicable)
- Customer or utility voltage drop and ampacity standards (often more restrictive than generic standards)

- DES (Design Engineer) — primary: performs wire sizing calculations, selects conductor types and sizes, documents justification
- Power System Engineer — provides load analysis, short-circuit current data, and system topology for circuit current and fault calculations

## Dependencies

### Hard Dependencies (Required Before Execution)

- SK-D03-001 ⏳: Load Flow and Power System Analysis — load analysis provides the steady-state current demand for each circuit
- SK-D04-001 ⏳: Functional Electrical Schematic Design — circuit specifications (voltage, current, signal levels) are derived from the functional schematic

### Soft Dependencies (Enhances Effectiveness)

- Power system short-circuit study or ETAP model — enables short-circuit withstand verification for high-current circuits
- SK-D06-002: Wiring Diagram Development — wiring diagrams incorporate the wire sizes selected by this skill
- SK-D06-004: Fabrication Drawing Production — fabrication drawings reference cable duct sizing based on wire gauge selection

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Every circuit in the functional schematic (SK-D04-001 ⏳) has a corresponding wire sizing calculation entry
2. Load currents used in calculations are traceable to equipment datasheets or power system load flow study
3. Wire gauge selection for every circuit is justified by both ampacity check and voltage drop check
4. Voltage drop for power circuits ≤ specified allowance (typical 3%); for control circuits ≤ 5% or per customer spec
5. For circuits in high short-circuit current zones: short-circuit withstand per IEC 60364-4-43 is verified (wire can safely carry the calculated fault current without melting or insulation failure)
6. Insulation type and voltage rating are appropriate for the application and environmental conditions
7. Color codes or conductor identification methods conform to IEC 60445 or applicable local standard
8. All derating factors (ambient temperature, conduit fill, altitude, if applicable) are explicitly documented and applied
9. Wire sizes and cable types are consistent across wiring diagram, terminal block schedule, and fabrication drawing (100% traceability)
10. Calculations have been reviewed and approved by the Design Engineer lead and reviewed by the Electrical Safety/Compliance Engineer for code compliance

## Description

Facilitate formal architecture review sessions per IEC 62443-3-2 and the ICP procedures document (ID01 §7.3.2), ensuring that system architecture designs are reviewed by qualified reviewers before proceeding to detailed design phases. Architecture reviews are a key governance and design quality assurance activity, providing independent verification that the proposed architecture meets the system requirements, complies with security and operational standards, and does not introduce unacceptable risks or design inconsistencies. This skill encompasses: scheduling and preparing review sessions, establishing reviewer roles and responsibilities, guiding design teams through structured review checklists (security-focused, operational, integration, and feasibility perspectives), documenting findings and observations, managing review follow-up to closure, and authorizing architecture design for advancement to the next phase. Architecture reviews serve as formal decision gates in the system development lifecycle, producing traceable evidence of design approval and enabling the organization to maintain design quality standards across multiple projects. This skill directly supports SK-D11-003 ⏳ (Critical Security Design Review), which applies heightened scrutiny to security-critical architectural decisions.

## Inputs

- System Architecture Design Documents (outputs from architecture design skills: SK-D02-001, SK-D02-003, SK-D02-006, SK-D02-007, SK-D02-008, etc.)
- System Requirements Specification (functional and non-functional requirements that the architecture must satisfy)
- Zone/Conduit Architecture Design (from SK-D01-001: Zone/Conduit Architecture Design)
- Preliminary Risk and Threat Assessment (from SK-D01-006 ⏳: Threat and Risk Assessment — Preliminary)
- Project Schedule and Resource Plan (to coordinate review scheduling)
- Organization's Architecture Review Procedure Documentation (review criteria, reviewer qualifications, escalation procedures)
- Design Review Checklist Templates (security, operational, integration, feasibility perspectives)

## Outputs

- Architecture Review Session Minutes (documented record of the review)
  - Review date, attendees, architecture design elements reviewed
  - Review questions asked and design team responses
  - Findings: deficiencies, risks, or inconsistencies identified
- Findings and Observations Report (detailed analysis of review findings)
  - Categorization: Critical (must be resolved before design approval), Major (significant risk or inconsistency), Minor (improvement suggestion)
  - Traceability: each finding linked to applicable requirement, standard, or architecture principle
  - Recommended corrective actions or design modifications
- Follow-up Action Register (tracking closure of review findings)
  - Action item description
  - Assigned owner
  - Target closure date
  - Status and closure evidence
- Architecture Design Approval Authorization (formal approval document authorizing design advancement to detailed design phase)
  - Signatures of qualified reviewers and PE/O
  - Explicit statement of design readiness and any approved exceptions or deferred items
- Review Metrics and Trending Data (number of findings by category, closure time, patterns across projects)

## Tools

- Facilitation: synchronous video conferencing (Zoom, Teams) for remote reviewer participation; in-person meeting rooms for co-located teams
- Documentation: shared design document repositories (Google Drive, SharePoint, Confluence)
- Review checklist management: structured templates (Excel, Confluence, Jira) with automated checklist scoring
- Meeting recording and transcription: Zoom recording, Otter.ai (for asynchronous review follow-up)

- Metrics dashboards: Tableau, Power BI (for tracking review metrics across projects)

## IEC 62443 Lifecycle Stages

- R2 (Detailed Design) — formal architecture review gate ensuring R1 security requirements are met and design is ready for R2 detailed design elaboration

## Roles

- SYS (System Architect) — primary: facilitates review sessions, guides reviewers through architecture documentation, tracks findings to closure
- SAC (Security Architect) — reviewer: evaluates architecture against security requirements and threat landscape
- DES (Design Engineer) — design team member: presents design rationale, responds to review questions
- STC (Security Technical Consultant) — reviewer: provides technical security perspective on protocols, encryption, authentication mechanisms
- INF (Infrastructure Architect) — reviewer: evaluates feasibility and operational implications
- QA (Quality Assurance) — reviewer: validates architecture against requirements specification, traces design to requirements
- PE/O (Project Engineer/Owner) — approver: authorizes design advancement, resolves escalations
- Independent Reviewer (external consultant or peer from another project) — reviewer: provides unbiased assessment of architecture quality

## Dependencies

### Hard Dependencies (Required Before Execution)

- At least one architecture design skill completed (e.g., SK-D02-001: System Partitioning and Functional Architecture) — must have a complete design artifact to review

### Soft Dependencies (Enhances Effectiveness)

- SK-D01-006 ⏳: Threat and Risk Assessment (Preliminary) — reviewers use threat landscape to evaluate architecture adequacy
- SK-D01-001: Zone/Conduit Architecture Design — security architecture design is a primary focus of architecture reviews
- Multiple architecture design skills completed in parallel (SK-D02-003, SK-D02-006, SK-D02-007, SK-D02-008) — comprehensive review of integrated architecture rather than individual components

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Review session is scheduled with all required reviewer roles represented (minimum: SAC, DES, QA, PE/O; recommend: STC, INF, independent reviewer)
2. All architecture design documents are provided to reviewers at least 3 business days before the review session for preparation
3. Review checklist is structured to address multiple perspectives: security (threat coverage, SL-T allocation), operational (availability, maintainability), integration (interface completeness), and feasibility (resource constraints)
4. Architecture design team presents the design with sufficient detail for reviewers to evaluate against requirements and standards
5. Review session documents all questions asked, design team responses, and identified findings
6. Findings are categorized (Critical/Major/Minor) and linked to applicable requirements or architecture principles
7. Corrective actions are assigned to responsible parties with target closure dates
8. Review findings closure is tracked to completion; evidence of resolution is documented
9. Architecture Design Approval Authorization is issued by PE/O only after Critical findings are resolved and Major findings have closure plans
10. Review metrics are captured and trends are monitored across projects to identify systemic architecture quality issues

| Junior (< 2 yr) | 2–4 person-days | Assumes single architecture design scope (e.g., one skill output), 3–5 reviewers, straightforward design |

Notes: Comprehensive architecture reviews covering multiple design skills (5+ outputs) may require 3–5 person-days for senior facilitation. Complex designs with significant findings may require 1–2 additional days for follow-up interaction and closure verification. Review preparation time by individual reviewers is separate effort (typically 4–8 hours per reviewer).

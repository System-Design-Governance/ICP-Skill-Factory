## Description

Develop the risk classification matrix that defines likelihood and impact scales, risk tolerance thresholds, and risk scoring methodology per IEC 62443-3-2. This provides the calibration framework used by SK-D01-006 (Preliminary Threat and Risk Assessment) and SK-D01-007 (Detailed Risk Assessment).

**Scope Boundary:** Matrix definition, scale calibration, and scoring methodology only. Actual risk assessment execution and risk value assignment to specific assets/zones falls under SK-D01-006 and SK-D01-007.

**Adjacent Skill References:**
- SK-D01-006: Preliminary Threat and Risk Assessment (downstream consumer)
- SK-D01-007: Detailed Risk Assessment (downstream consumer)
- SK-D01-009: Risk Classification Matrix Development (this skill)

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Likelihood scale includes both qualitative descriptors and quantitative probability ranges (e.g., frequency per year, percentage).
2. Impact scale defines a minimum of four dimensions: business/financial, safety, operational/availability, reputation/compliance.
3. Risk tolerance thresholds are explicitly stated in the matrix (e.g., unacceptable, tolerable with controls, acceptable risk ranges).
4. Risk scoring formula is mathematically defined and documented (e.g., Risk = Likelihood × Impact with weighting if applicable).
5. Matrix includes visual mapping (color coding or numbering) of risk levels to clearly indicate severity categories.
6. Calibration notes document the rationale for likelihood/impact ranges and tolerance thresholds.
7. The matrix is reviewed and approved by Security Architect, Risk Manager, and at least one Stakeholder Representative.
8. The matrix includes guidance on how to apply the scale consistently across different asset types and zones.

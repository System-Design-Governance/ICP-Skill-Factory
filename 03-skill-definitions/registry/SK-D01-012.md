## Description

Execute comprehensive security audits of the OT/ICS automation solution (SuC) during the operational phase (R4) to verify ongoing compliance with IEC 62443 requirements and organizational security policies. This skill establishes a formal audit process that systematically evaluates the effectiveness of deployed security controls, documents findings, recommends corrective actions, and generates audit reports for stakeholder review. Audits are conducted per IEC 62443-2-1 §7.8.3.1 security audit procedures, serve as key feedback loops for continuous improvement, and establish evidence of due diligence for regulatory and governance oversight. This skill builds on SK-D01-011 (IEC 62443 Compliance Gap Analysis) and verification activities from R1–R3 phases.

## Description — Objectives

- Conduct systematic security audits to verify deployed controls meet IEC 62443 requirements and organizational policies
- Plan audit scope, objectives, and methodology per ID01 §7.8.3.1 security audit requirements
- Collect audit evidence (configuration reviews, testing, interviews, log analysis, inspection)
- Identify audit findings (control effectiveness gaps, non-compliance, risk exposures)
- Document findings in audit report with severity classification and corrective action recommendations
- Establish audit schedule, resource allocation, and independence of audit team
- Maintain audit documentation and evidence chain for governance review and process improvement

## Description — Scope

**In Scope:** Audit planning and objective definition; control effectiveness assessment against IEC 62443 requirements and organizational security policies; evidence collection methodology; finding documentation with root cause and remediation recommendation; audit report generation; post-audit follow-up and remediation tracking.

**Out of Scope:** Implementation of corrective actions (assigned to responsible business unit); strategic policy development (handled by SK-D01-030); operational incident response (separate operational process); continuous monitoring dashboards (handled by SK-D01-018).

- IEC 62443-2-1 security program requirements (ID01), including §7.8.3.1 audit procedures

## Description — Key Concepts

- **Security Audit:** Systematic, documented examination of security controls and compliance with established requirements and organizational policies
- **Control Effectiveness:** The extent to which a deployed security control achieves its intended objective and reduces the target risk
- **Audit Finding:** A documented observation that a control is absent, incomplete, improperly configured, or ineffectively operated
- **Corrective Action:** A remediation measure recommended to address an audit finding and restore control effectiveness
- **Audit Evidence:** Objective information (logs, configurations, test results, interviews, documentation) used to support audit conclusions
- **Audit Independence:** The audit team operates free from pressure or conflict of interest; may be internal (company) or external (third-party) auditors

## Description — Methodology

Security audit execution follows a structured five-phase process:

**Phase 1: Audit Planning**
- Define audit objectives (e.g., "verify zone boundary controls comply with approved architecture and IEC 62443-3-3 SR 2.1")
- Establish audit scope (assets, systems, locations, control domains to be assessed)
- Develop audit criteria based on IEC 62443-2-1 §7.8.3.1 and organizational security policies
- Assess risk and resource requirements; allocate audit team and schedule
- Obtain management approval and target environment access
- Prepare audit work programs (step-by-step procedures for control testing)

**Phase 2: Control Assessment**
- Conduct control walkthroughs with responsible personnel to understand design and operation
- Review control documentation (policies, procedures, configuration baselines, change logs)
- Perform evidence collection:
  - Configuration audits (firewall rules, RBAC settings, encryption settings)
  - Testing (access control lists, authentication mechanisms, audit logging)
  - Log analysis (authentication events, privilege escalation, security events)
  - Physical inspection (secure room access, cable routing, hardware protection)
  - Interviews with operators, engineers, and security personnel
- Classify control effectiveness: Compliant, Partially Compliant, Non-Compliant, or Not Applicable

**Phase 3: Finding Documentation**
- Document each finding with:
  - Control ID and requirement reference (IEC 62443 SR or organizational policy)
  - Observation (what was found or not found)
  - Root cause analysis (why the control is ineffective)
  - Risk exposure (what risk the finding creates)
  - Severity classification (Critical, High, Medium, Low) based on risk and compliance impact
- Distinguish between facts (directly observed) and conclusions (interpreted findings)
- Provide supporting evidence citations (audit logs, testing records, documentation reviewed)

**Phase 4: Corrective Action Recommendation**
- For each finding, recommend specific, actionable corrective action
- Recommend owner and target completion date
- Estimate effort and resource requirements
- Prioritize recommendations by severity and risk mitigation value
- Validate recommendations with responsible business unit before including in audit report

**Phase 5: Audit Report and Follow-up**
- Prepare formal Audit Report including:
  - Executive Summary (audit objective, scope, period, findings count by severity)
  - Detailed Findings (with corrective action recommendations)
  - Compliance Summary (percentage of controls effective, gaps by domain)
  - Improvement Roadmap (sequenced corrective actions with ownership and dates)
- Present audit report to governance/steering committee
- Establish corrective action tracking dashboard
- Conduct periodic follow-up audits (per schedule) to verify closure of prior-year findings

## Description — Activities

1. Establish audit charter and define authority, scope, and independence per ID01 §7.8.3.1
2. Develop annual audit plan with defined audit schedule and resource allocation
3. For each scheduled audit:
   - Develop detailed audit program (step-by-step control testing procedures)
   - Conduct pre-audit coordination with target environment stakeholders
   - Execute control walkthroughs and evidence collection per audit work program
   - Hold closing conference with auditees to discuss preliminary findings and factual accuracy
4. Consolidate findings into audit report with recommended corrective actions
5. Present audit report to governance with executive briefing
6. Establish corrective action tracking and periodic follow-up audits to verify remediation
7. Maintain audit documentation (work papers, evidence logs, management responses, remediation status) per organizational record retention policy

## Outputs

- **Audit Charter:** Formal authorization document defining audit scope, authority, independence, and standards (per ID01 §7.8.3.1)
- **Annual Audit Plan:** Schedule of planned audits, resource allocation, and audit objectives for each scheduled audit
- **Detailed Audit Program:** Step-by-step testing procedures for each control assessment
- **Audit Work Papers:** Documented evidence (testing logs, configuration snapshots, interview notes, log extracts)
- **Formal Audit Report:** Executive summary, detailed findings with severity classification, corrective action recommendations, and compliance roadmap
- **Corrective Action Tracking Dashboard:** Status tracking of open findings and management responses with target closure dates
- **Management Letter:** Summary findings and recommendations for senior leadership
- **Follow-up Audit Report:** Verification of corrective action effectiveness and residual findings

## Acceptance Criteria

- Audit plan is documented and approved by governance; audits are conducted per established schedule
- All control assessments are performed per defined audit program with documented evidence supporting each conclusion
- Every finding is documented with: observation, root cause, risk exposure, severity classification, and corrective action recommendation
- Findings are classified by severity (Critical/High/Medium/Low) with documented justification
- Audit report is formally issued and presented to governance/steering committee
- Every finding receives documented management response with owner assignment, target date, and proposed corrective action
- Corrective action tracking is maintained through closure with evidence of remediation
- Follow-up audits verify effectiveness of corrective actions and identify closure of prior-year findings
- Audit independence is maintained; audit team does not have conflicting operational responsibilities
- Audit documentation is retained per organizational records policy and is available for regulatory/governance review

## Description — Practical Guidance

- **Pitfall:** Conducting audits without defined scope or criteria. **Guidance:** Establish detailed audit program and clear criteria (IEC 62443-2-1, organizational policy) before fieldwork begins; obtain management approval of audit scope.
- **Pitfall:** Failing to distinguish between facts (observed evidence) and conclusions (interpretations). **Guidance:** Document exactly what was observed; clearly separate facts from conclusions; reference supporting evidence for each finding.
- **Pitfall:** Allowing audit team to have operational responsibilities that compromise independence. **Guidance:** Ensure audit team is segregated from daily operations; address conflicts of interest at audit planning stage; document independence in audit charter.
- **Pitfall:** Identifying findings without root cause or corrective action recommendations. **Guidance:** For each finding, conduct root cause analysis and recommend specific, actionable corrective measures with owner and target date.
- **Pitfall:** Conducting one-time audits without follow-up verification. **Guidance:** Establish periodic follow-up audits per ID01 §7.8.3.1 frequency requirements; track corrective action closure and verify remediation effectiveness.
- **Pitfall:** Using vague severity classifications. **Guidance:** Define severity criteria based on control effectiveness impact and compliance significance; ensure objective, consistent application across all findings.

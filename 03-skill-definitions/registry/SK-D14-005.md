## Description

Develop the Commercial Bill of Materials (CBOM) for the proposed OT/ICS cybersecurity solution, itemizing all hardware, software, licenses, and services required for delivery and establishing the commercial cost baseline for the bid/quotation. Under GOV-SD BOUNDARY-010, the CBOM is commercially quotable but design non-binding — the System Architect at Gate 1 can adjust or reject CBOM items without violating the commercial commitment. The CBOM status flow is: Draft → Quoted → Gate 0 Approved. The CBOM↔EBOM delta (where Engineering BOM diverges from Commercial BOM) is business unit responsibility, not design department responsibility. This skill covers CBOM line-item development and cost estimation; the labor hour estimation that feeds the CBOM services section falls under SK-D14-006, and the cost risk contingency analysis falls under SK-D14-016 ⏳.

## Inputs

- Requirements specification (from SK-D14-001) — scope of the solution to be costed
- Feasibility assessment (from SK-D14-003) — technology selections and architectural approach
- Preliminary security classification (from SK-D14-014) — SL-T levels drive security product selection
- Existing infrastructure inventory (from SK-D14-012) — what can be reused vs. what must be procured
- Labor hour estimation (from SK-D14-006) — services cost input
- Vendor pricing: hardware, software, license, and maintenance cost data
- ICP standard pricing templates (CF-SR-03-01 or similar BOM template)
- Currency handling rules (per ICP CBOM conventions)

## Outputs

- Commercial Bill of Materials (CBOM) Document:
  - Hardware line items: servers, network switches, firewalls, endpoint devices, cables, cabinets — per item: quantity, unit price, extended price, lead time
  - Software line items: operating systems, security software (antivirus, SIEM, vulnerability scanner), application licenses — per item: license type (perpetual/subscription), quantity, unit price, renewal terms
  - Service line items: engineering services (design, implementation, testing, commissioning), training, documentation — linked to labor hour estimation (SK-D14-006)
  - Maintenance/support line items: annual maintenance, vendor support contracts, spare parts — per item: annual cost, contract term
  - Cost summary: total hardware, software, services, maintenance, contingency, total project cost
  - Currency handling: primary currency, exchange rate assumptions, multi-currency line items where applicable
  - CBOM status: Draft / Quoted / Gate 0 Approved (per GOV-SD status flow)
- CBOM Assumptions Document: pricing assumptions, vendor quotation validity dates, quantity-dependent pricing tiers, exchange rate assumptions
- Security Cost Breakdown: security-specific items extracted from CBOM for tender security requirements alignment

## Automation Potential

**Level:** Partial

BOM template population from product databases can be automated (per cbom-builder skill). Standard pricing lookup and cost calculation can be automated. Currency conversion and exchange rate application can be automated. However, product selection requires engineering judgment (matching requirements to specific vendor products), vendor negotiation and quotation management requires human interaction, and pricing assumptions require commercial context that varies per project.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. CBOM covers all four cost categories: hardware, software, services, and maintenance — no category empty without documented rationale
2. Every line item has: description, quantity, unit price, extended price, and source (vendor quote / estimated / historical) — no items with missing pricing
3. CBOM status clearly marked (Draft / Quoted / Gate 0 Approved) per GOV-SD status flow
4. CBOM marked as commercially quotable but design non-binding per BOUNDARY-010 — disclaimer language included
5. Pricing assumptions documented: vendor quotation validity dates, exchange rate assumptions, quantity-dependent pricing noted
6. Security cost breakdown extractable: security-specific line items identifiable for tender security cost alignment

## Estimated Effort

| Level | Effort | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Junior (< 2 yr) | 5–8 person-days | Assumes medium-complexity project, ~50 line items; includes product research, vendor quotation, cost calculation, and documentation |
| Senior (5+ yr) | 3–5 person-days | Same scope; senior leverages pricing databases and vendor relationships |

Notes: Effort scales with solution complexity and vendor count. Multi-vendor solutions with many product categories increase effort. Tight tender deadlines may require parallel vendor quotation processes. CBOM accuracy improves with vendor quotation availability — budget pricing is faster but less accurate. CBOM is iterated: initial draft may be refined 2–3 times before quotation submission.

## Description

This skill enables the Head of System Design to collect, evaluate, and score performance evidence for the 7 GOV-SDP engineering roles using the SMART KPI (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound) framework and RCW/AF/PVF scoring formula (Role Complexity Weight × Achievement Factor × Performance Variance Factor). The skill operationalizes the GOV-SDP performance management system, producing per-role scorecards that feed into organizational bonus mechanisms and competency development planning.

## Standards

IEC 62443-2-1 (Security Management System — Performance Management and Competency Assessment), GOV-SDP governance framework (Tier 1: SMART KPI definition, RCW/AF/PVF scoring formula, engineering role performance assessment, bonus mechanism).

## IEC 62443 Lifecycle Stages

R0 (Planning), R1 (Requirements), R2 (Design), R3 (Implementation), R4 (Verification), R5 (Operations)

## Acceptance Criteria

### SMART KPI Framework Implementation
- For each of the 7 GOV-SDP engineering roles (System Architect, Security Architect, Requirements Engineer, Design Engineer, Verification Engineer, Implementation Engineer, Operations Engineer), SMART KPI objectives are defined.
- Each KPI is Specific (clear, unambiguous goal), Measurable (quantifiable or objectively verifiable), Achievable (realistic within role scope), Relevant (aligned with project/organizational objectives), and Time-bound (defined timeline: quarterly, semi-annual, annual).
- KPI objectives distinguish between role-specific technical excellence (design correctness, verification completeness, implementation quality) and cross-functional contributions (collaboration, knowledge sharing, mentoring).
- KPI definitions are documented per role with success criteria and measurement methodology.

### Evidence Collection Procedures
- Evidence collection procedures are defined per KPI with data sources identified (project artifacts, code reviews, test reports, design review minutes, verification reports, stakeholder feedback).
- Evidence is collected throughout the performance period using standardized templates to ensure consistency and traceability.
- Evidence collection responsibilities are assigned to appropriate evaluators (peer review, management assessment, stakeholder input).
- Evidence is documented with collection date, source, and link to KPI objective.
- Evidence is retained in organized repository for scorecard preparation and performance audits.

### RCW (Role Complexity Weight) Assessment
- Role Complexity Weight (RCW) is determined per role based on technical scope, decision authority, and security responsibility.
- RCW values are assigned per GOV-SDP weighting scale (e.g., RCW values: 1.0 for foundational roles, 1.5 for mid-tier roles, 2.0 for lead/architect roles).
- RCW assessment considers: breadth of technical domain covered, depth of security/criticality responsibility, scope of cross-team coordination, and complexity of decision-making required.
- RCW is documented with assessment rationale and reviewed annually for continued appropriateness.

### AF (Achievement Factor) Calculation
- Achievement Factor (AF) is calculated per KPI based on evidence evaluation against success criteria.
- AF uses ordinal scale reflecting performance level: AF = 0.0 (not met), 0.5 (partially met), 1.0 (met), 1.25 (exceeded), 1.5 (significantly exceeded).
- AF is assigned by evaluators based on documented evidence and assessed against success criteria.
- AF assignments are reviewed for consistency across roles and evaluators to ensure fairness.
- AF values are consolidated per role to produce overall achievement score.

### PVF (Performance Variance Factor) Adjustment
- Performance Variance Factor (PVF) adjusts the raw RCW × AF score based on contextual project/organizational factors.
- PVF factors include: project complexity variance (scope increases, technical challenges), resource availability constraints (staffing changes, skill gaps), external factor impacts (client changes, regulatory impacts).
- PVF is documented with specific factors influencing adjustment and approved by Head of System Design.
- PVF adjustments are applied consistently across roles and transparently communicated to affected personnel.
- PVF values typically range 0.8 to 1.2 reflecting unfavorable to favorable performance context.

### Role KPI Score Calculation
- Role KPI Score = RCW × AF × PVF, calculated per role for each performance period.
- Individual KPI scores are calculated and then consolidated to role-level summary scores.
- Scores are documented with supporting evidence summary and evaluation rationale.
- Score calculations are reviewed for mathematical accuracy and reasonableness checks (e.g., scores within expected ranges).

### Performance Scorecard Development
- Performance scorecard is prepared per role documenting: role definition, KPI objectives, evidence collected, AF assignments, RCW, PVF adjustment, final RCW × AF × PVF score, and interpretive narrative.
- Scorecard includes comparisons to prior performance period to identify trends and development areas.
- Scorecard is documented in standardized template format for consistency across roles.
- Scorecard is reviewed with evaluated personnel for accuracy confirmation and discussion of performance results.

### Bonus Mechanism Calculation
- Role KPI scores are mapped to bonus mechanism per GOV-SDP bonus formula (bonus percentage tied to score ranges and organizational performance).
- Individual role scores contribute to aggregate team/project performance scoring.
- Bonus payments are calculated based on approved bonus formula and documented with calculations.
- Bonus calculations are reviewed for consistency with organizational compensation policy.

### Competency Development Planning Integration
- KPI scorecard results are analyzed to identify competency gaps and development opportunities per SK-D11-012.
- Underperformance in specific KPI areas triggers competency assessment and development planning.
- Development plans include training, mentoring, stretch assignments, or role adjustments.
- Development plan progress is tracked and incorporated into subsequent performance evaluation cycles.

## Description — Integration Notes

Role KPI Evidence Collection & Scoring operationalizes the GOV-SDP performance management system, translating organizational strategy into individual role-level performance expectations. The SMART KPI framework ensures measurable, objective performance assessment. The RCW/AF/PVF formula provides transparent, consistent scoring across diverse roles and performance periods. KPI scores directly inform bonus mechanisms and competency development planning (SK-D11-012), creating feedback loops between performance assessment and professional development. The Head of System Design must ensure evaluator calibration and fairness across evaluations. Results must be communicated transparently to personnel with clear linkage to organizational and project objectives. The skill requires integration with HR and compensation systems for accurate bonus mechanism implementation. Performance trends must inform risk identification and resource planning (SK-D10-001, SK-D10-005).

## Description

The Permit to Work (PtW) Process Management skill establishes and maintains formal authorization procedures for controlling access to operational OT/ICS systems during commissioning, maintenance, and modification activities. This capability implements risk-based permit authorization aligned with ID04 §12.0 requirements, covering multiple permit types (hot work, electrical work, confined space entry) and their associated risk assessment protocols. The process creates an audit trail that tracks work activities from authorization through permit closure and system state restoration, ensuring that only authorized personnel perform designated work within defined scopes and risk parameters. By integrating permit management with operational risk assessment, this skill prevents unauthorized modifications and maintains system integrity across the lifecycle of maintenance and commissioning operations.

## IEC 62443 Lifecycle Stages

- **R0 (Initiation)**: Project initiation and authorization framework definition
- **R1 (Concept)**: Permit types and risk categories identified; process flow designed
- **R2 (Definition)**: Formal PtW procedure and templates documented; approval chains established
- **R3 (Implementation)**: Process deployed; stakeholders trained; tracking systems operational
- **R4 (Verification)**: Permit closure verification and audit trails confirmed; compliance audited

2. All four permit types (hot work, electrical, confined space, system modification) are covered with complete templates and risk assessment protocols

5. Process is operationally feasible within project timelines and scales to organizational maintenance demands
6. Audit trail capability captures all permit lifecycle events with timestamp and role attribution

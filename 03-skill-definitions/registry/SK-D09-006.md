## Description

This skill encompasses the authoring of comprehensive Operation Manuals for OT/ICS systems, written specifically for plant operators and maintenance technicians rather than engineers. The manual must cover system overview and architecture relevant to operators, step-by-step startup and shutdown procedures, normal operation procedures including setpoint changes and mode transitions, alarm response and troubleshooting procedures, and security-relevant operational procedures (e.g., password changes, access control usage, incident reporting). The skill emphasizes clarity, accessibility, and safety—every procedure must be written in plain language with illustrations, warnings, and verification steps. Operation Manuals are a contractual deliverable per ID03 §5.5.3 and a critical component of operational readiness prior to system handover to the customer.

## Inputs

- System functional design specification and control logic (from SK-D05-001: Control System Functional Specification)
- HMI screen design and operator interface specifications (from SK-D05-005: HMI Screen Design)
- Alarm and event list with response procedures (from SK-D05-010: Alarm and Event Management)
- System safety analysis and operational hazards assessment (from SK-D01-018: Safety Instrumented System Design)
- Security operational procedures and access control policies (from SK-D01-020: Account and Access Control)
- Startup/shutdown sequences from commissioning plan (from SK-D07-001: Commissioning Plan)
- BOM and hardware specifications for operator reference
- Lessons learned from similar system operations (if available)

## Outputs

- Operation Manual: comprehensive document organized by procedure type (startup, normal operation, alarm response, troubleshooting, shutdown, security procedures)
- Quick Reference Cards: laminated or digital single-page procedure summaries for critical operations (startup, emergency shutdown, critical alarms)
- Operator Procedure Checklist: step-by-step checklist format for operators to verify procedure completion
- Troubleshooting Decision Tree: visual flowcharts for diagnosing and resolving common operational issues
- Glossary and Abbreviation Reference: definitions of technical terms and system-specific abbreviations
- Appendices: alarm tables, setpoint reference, contact information for support, spare parts lists

## Tools

- Microsoft Word with templates for technical documentation
- Visio or Draw.io for procedure flowcharts and troubleshooting trees
- MadCap Flare or ClickHelp (for online searchable documentation with version control)
- Plain language writing tools and readability checkers

- Markdown editors with version control (Git) for collaborative authoring

## IEC 62443 Lifecycle Stages

- R3 (Implementation) — Operation Manuals are prepared during implementation and refined during commissioning
- R4 (Commissioning/Startup) — manuals are tested with actual operators and refined based on commissioning feedback
- R5 (Operations) — manuals are the primary reference for plant operators during operations and maintenance

## Roles

- DES (Design Engineer) — primary: gathers operational procedures from design artifacts and composes manual
- OPS (Operations Lead or Plant Manager) — reviews manual for operational accuracy and operator audience appropriateness
- STC (Security Technical Consultant) — reviews security operational procedures section for completeness and alignment
- TMS (Training and Materials Specialist) — may be involved for technical writing quality and usability testing with operators
- Operator (representative) — reviews draft manual and provides feedback on clarity and practicality

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Operation Manual covers all operational modes and procedures: startup, normal operation, mode transitions, alarm response, troubleshooting, and shutdown
2. Every alarm listed in the Alarm and Event Management register has a documented response procedure in the manual with clear action steps
3. Manual is written in plain language appropriate for plant operators; technical terms are defined; setpoint values and limits are clearly specified
4. All security-relevant operational procedures (access control, password management, incident reporting) are documented with step-by-step instructions
5. Manual includes illustrations, screenshots, or flowcharts for at least 80% of procedures; critical procedures have visual aids
6. Manual has been reviewed and approved by the Operations Lead and at least one plant operator; feedback has been incorporated

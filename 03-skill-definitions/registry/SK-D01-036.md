## Description

This skill enables the Security Architect to maintain comprehensive risk source traceability from initial identification through mitigation to residual risk acceptance. It establishes a standardized risk identifier system and residual risk register that documents all risks remaining after countermeasure implementation, supporting Gate 3 verification with 20% sampling and structured residual risk acceptance through the organizational hierarchy.

## Standards

IEC 62443-3-3 (System Security Requirements and Impact Analysis, Risk Assessment, Countermeasure Implementation and Verification), IEC 62443-4-1 (SD-2: Risk Assessment and Countermeasure Analysis), GOV-SD governance framework (Tier 1: Gate 3 verification and risk acceptance authority hierarchy).

## IEC 62443 Lifecycle Stages

R1 (Requirements), R2 (Design), R3 (Implementation)

## Acceptance Criteria

### Risk Identifier System Implementation
- Risk source identifiers are assigned using standardized prefixes per GOV-SD: T-XXX for IEC 62443 threat catalog (T-001 through T-NNN), FM-XXX for FMEA failure modes (FM-001 through FM-NNN), HAZ-XXX for HAZOP process deviations (HAZ-001 through HAZ-NNN).
- Risk identifiers are tracked in the risk registry with creation date, originating analysis method, and assigned security architect.
- Identifier system is applied consistently across all risk identification sources (threat analysis, FMEA, HAZOP, design review).

### Risk Traceability Chain Establishment
- For each risk identifier, the complete traceability chain is documented: risk source → risk characteristics (likelihood, impact, initial severity) → assigned countermeasures → implementation status → residual risk assessment.
- Risk traceability records include cross-references to design artifacts (threat model, FMEA analysis, HAZOP study, architecture documents, countermeasure specifications).
- Relationships between related risks (dependencies, cascading effects, risk combinations) are identified and documented.
- Version control and change history are maintained for all risk traceability records.

### Residual Risk Register Maintenance
- Residual risk register documents all risks remaining after countermeasure implementation with residual likelihood, residual impact, residual severity, and risk acceptance status.
- Residual risk entries include: risk identifier, original risk description, implemented countermeasures, residual risk characterization, acceptance authority, acceptance date, and risk owner.
- Residual risk severity is re-evaluated based on countermeasure effectiveness evidence from testing/verification.
- Register is maintained in version-controlled repository with audit trail of all modifications.

### Gate 3 Verification Sampling (20%)
- Gate 3 verification sampling plan documents 20% of total risks for detailed verification of countermeasure effectiveness.
- Sampling strategy ensures proportional coverage across risk categories (threats, failure modes, hazards) and severity levels.
- For sampled risks, verification evidence is collected from test reports, design review records, and operational data.
- Verification findings are documented with pass/fail status for each sampled risk's countermeasure effectiveness.

### Residual Risk Acceptance Hierarchy
- Residual risks are presented to appropriate acceptance authority per organizational hierarchy:
  - PM (Project Manager) only: residual severity ≤ Low
  - PM + Security (SAC/ISM): residual severity = Medium
  - PM + Security + Business (Executive Steering Committee): residual severity = High
  - Engineering Management + All Above: residual severity = Critical (requires Root Cause Analysis and alternative controls)
- Risk acceptance decisions are documented with authorization signatures and acceptance rationale.
- Conditions of acceptance (operational constraints, compensating controls, risk reviews) are defined.
- Accepted residual risks are communicated to relevant stakeholders with risk owner assignment.

### Risk Owner Assignment & Monitoring
- Each residual risk is assigned a risk owner responsible for monitoring, reporting, and escalation.
- Risk owners are assigned based on technical domain and authority level required for management.
- Risk owner responsibilities, escalation triggers, and reporting frequency are defined.
- Periodic risk reviews (quarterly minimum) are conducted to assess residual risk status and identify emerging issues.

## Description — Integration Notes

Risk source traceability and residual risk register form the foundation of defensible security assurance. The standardized identifier system (T-XXX, FM-XXX, HAZ-XXX) enables cross-functional traceability across threat analysis, FMEA, and HAZOP disciplines. The Gate 3 sampling requirement ensures verification proportionality while maintaining detailed traceability for all risks. The residual risk acceptance hierarchy distributes decision-making authority based on risk severity and organizational governance. Risk owners must monitor residual risks throughout operational lifetime, escalating changes to risk profile through formal change control (SK-D10-002, SK-D10-003).

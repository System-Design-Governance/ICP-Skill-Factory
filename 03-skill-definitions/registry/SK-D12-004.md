## Description

This skill encompasses the design of data retention policies that specify how long operational data, security logs, and audit data will be retained, at what granularity (raw vs. aggregated), in what storage medium (hot/active storage, cold/archive storage), and when data will be securely deleted. Data retention policies must balance competing requirements: regulatory compliance (GDPR, data protection laws, industry-specific mandates), forensic investigation timelines (for security incident root cause analysis), cost optimization (storage expenses increase with retention duration), and operational utility (older data may be less frequently queried). The policy coordinates with data classification (SK-D01-033: Data Classification Policy) to define retention periods by sensitivity level, and with database design (SK-D12-003: Time-Series Database Selection) to inform storage tiering and archival strategies.

## Inputs

- Data Classification Policy (from SK-D01-033: Data Classification Policy) — data sensitivity levels drive retention period decisions
- Regulatory and Compliance Requirements: GDPR, HIPAA, industry-specific regulations (NERC for utilities, pipeline safety standards for energy)
- Forensic Investigation Requirements: incident response timeline and root cause analysis needs (from SK-D01-024: Incident Response Planning)
- Data Acquisition Architecture (from SK-D12-001: Data Acquisition Architecture) — identifies data types and volumes requiring retention decisions
- Time-Series Database Capabilities (from SK-D12-003: Time-Series Database Selection) — database compression, archival, and tiering capabilities
- Organizational data governance policies and storage capacity constraints
- Cost and budget considerations for long-term storage and archival

## Outputs

- Data Retention Policy Document: policy statement specifying retention periods by data classification, archival procedures, deletion schedules, and secure disposal methods
- Data Retention Matrix: table showing data element/type, classification level, minimum retention period, archival method, and deletion trigger
- Storage Tiering Plan: design for hot storage (active queryable data), warm storage (less frequently accessed), and cold storage (archive) with transition criteria and costs
- Archival and Compression Strategy: procedures for transitioning data from active storage to compressed archive, with searchability and recovery procedures
- Secure Deletion Procedures: methods for permanently and securely deleting data when retention period expires (cryptographic erasure, physical destruction procedures)
- Compliance Mapping: alignment of retention policy with applicable regulatory requirements with evidence of compliance

## IEC 62443 Lifecycle Stages

- R2 (Detailed Design) — data retention policy is designed and aligned with data classification
- R3 (Implementation) — retention policies are configured in database and storage systems
- R4 (Commissioning/Startup) — retention policies are tested with actual data volumes and verified during commissioning
- R5 (Operations) — retention policies are enforced through database configuration, monitoring, and periodic secure deletion runs

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Data Retention Policy specifies retention periods (in days, months, or years) for each data classification level; retention periods are justified by regulatory, operational, or forensic requirements documented in the policy rationale
2. Retention Matrix clearly maps each data type to: classification level, minimum retention period, archival trigger (automatic or manual), storage tier transition (hot → warm → cold), and deletion procedure/timeline
3. Storage Tiering Plan specifies cost implications and searchability for each tier; transition procedures from active to archive to deletion are documented with criteria and automation approach
4. Secure deletion procedures are specified for all data types, including cryptographic erasure (if applicable), overwrite procedures, or physical destruction for archived media; procedures align with relevant security standards (NIST, DoD, etc.)
5. Compliance Mapping document shows alignment of retention policy with all applicable regulatory requirements (GDPR, industry-specific, organizational); evidence of compliance is documented
6. Retention Policy has been reviewed and approved by Security Architect, Compliance Officer, and Database Administrator; technical feasibility in selected database platform is confirmed

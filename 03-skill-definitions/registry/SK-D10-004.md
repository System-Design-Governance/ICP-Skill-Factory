## Description

This skill enables the project manager to facilitate technical clarification meetings between the ICP project team and customer/stakeholders. These meetings resolve ambiguities in requirements, design decisions, and implementation approach through structured dialogue and documented technical decisions.

## Standards

IEC 62443-4-1 (SD-2: Systems Security Requirements), IEC 62443-3-3 (System Security Audit), and organizational governance standards for project management and technical coordination.

## IEC 62443 Lifecycle Stages

R1 (Requirements), R2 (Design), R3 (Implementation)

## Acceptance Criteria

### Agenda Preparation
- Technical clarification meeting agenda is prepared with identified ambiguity items, stakeholder list, pre-meeting documents review, and success criteria documented.
- Ambiguity items are classified by impact area (requirements, design, implementation, schedule, budget, risk).
- Meeting duration, attendees, and scope are agreed upon in advance with customer/stakeholder.

### Meeting Facilitation
- Meeting facilitator guides discussion to systematically address each agenda item with structured Q&A.
- Technical clarification is recorded in real-time with agreed-upon terminology and decision rationales.
- Conflicting viewpoints are acknowledged, discussed, and resolved or escalated with documented reasoning.
- Meeting outcome is summarized with participants confirming understanding of clarifications and decisions.

### Action Item Tracking
- Action items are assigned to responsible parties with defined due dates, priority levels, and success criteria.
- Action items are tracked in the project management system with status updates at defined intervals.
- Completion evidence for action items is collected and verified before closure.

### Technical Clarification Record Documentation
- Technical clarification records document the original ambiguity, discussion summary, clarified position, decision rationale, and affected artifacts.
- Records are traceable to customer/stakeholder acceptance and cross-referenced with requirements (SK-D14-001), change requests (SK-D10-002), and design decision records (SK-D02-009).
- Documentation is version-controlled and retained per organizational records retention policy.

## Inputs

- Customer/stakeholder requirements documentation with identified ambiguities
- Project schedule and resource availability
- Design and implementation approach documentation (if available)
- Previous technical clarification records and meeting minutes
- Risk register and change log

## Outputs

- Technical clarification meeting agenda (with pre-meeting information package)
- Technical clarification records (ambiguity → clarification mapping)
- Action items list with assignments and tracking status
- Meeting minutes with decisions, decisions rationale, and attendee sign-off
- Stakeholder acceptance confirmation of clarifications

## Roles — Competency Requirements

- Technical communication and facilitation skills
- Understanding of project management principles (scheduling, resource planning)
- Familiarity with ICP architecture, design approach, and technical standards
- Stakeholder management and negotiation skills
- Ability to synthesize technical information and document decisions

## Estimated Effort

- Percentage of identified ambiguities resolved in first meeting
- Time to closure of action items assigned during meeting
- Stakeholder satisfaction with clarification quality and documentation
- Traceability of clarified decisions to downstream artifacts (requirements, design, change control)

## Description — Integration Notes

Technical clarification meetings serve as a quality gate between customer/stakeholder expectations and engineering deliverables, preventing requirements ambiguity from propagating into design and implementation. These meetings must coordinate with change management (SK-D10-002) to distinguish clarifications (no scope change) from scope changes (formal change control).

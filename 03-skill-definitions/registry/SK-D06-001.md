## Description

Design control panel physical layouts including component placement, cable routing, thermal management, and ergonomic considerations for electrical control cabinets and enclosures. This skill covers spatial arrangement of components (breakers, contactors, relays, PLCs, power supplies, terminals) to optimize accessibility, maintenance, and cooling; cable tray and conduit routing to minimize electromagnetic interference and facilitate troubleshooting; and thermal analysis to ensure adequate cooling capacity (natural convection, forced air cooling, or active climate control). Thermal calculations verify cooling adequacy per IEC 62208 (Safety of Machinery — Graphical Symbols for Operator Controls and Signalling Devices — also addresses thermal dissipation guidelines) and NEMA 4/4X/12K ratings for enclosure type. Component selection constraints from SK-D06-006 (Component Selection and Procurement) feed directly into layout constraints, ensuring compatibility with panel footprint and thermal envelope. The output is a detailed panel layout drawing and thermal validation report.

## Inputs

- Equipment and component list from SK-D06-006: Component Selection and Procurement (including power ratings, thermal dissipation, dimensions, mounting requirements)
- System Architecture Design and functional requirements (from SK-D05-001 ⏳ and SK-D05-002 ⏳)
- Electrical single-line diagram and control wiring schematic (from SK-D06-002 ⏳: Single-Line Diagram and Control Wiring Design)
- Panel environmental specifications: installation location (indoor/outdoor), ambient temperature range, humidity, IP/NEMA rating requirement
- Customer panel standard or guidelines (if applicable)
- Cabling specifications and voltage drop calculations (from SK-D06-002 ⏳)
- Ergonomic requirements: operator panel height, indicator lamp/button arrangement, ventilation/cooling access
- Maintenance access requirements and service plan

## Outputs

- Panel Layout Drawing (CAD format, typically AutoCAD or equivalent): top, front, side, and interior views showing component locations, mounting heights, cable tray routing, terminal block arrangements
- Component Placement Table: detailed location, orientation, and mounting method for each component with reference to electrical drawings
- Thermal Calculation and Analysis Report: steady-state heat dissipation calculation per IEC 62208, cooling method selection (natural/forced/active), verification that enclosure temperature remains within operational limits
- Cable Routing Diagram: conduit/tray layout showing separation of power, control, and signal cables; grounding path routing; electromagnetic compatibility measures
- Panel Assembly Procedure: step-by-step instructions for component mounting, wiring, testing, and acceptance
- Thermal Management Specification: cooling system selection (fan size/capacity, filter maintenance intervals, thermostat setpoints if active climate control)
- Bill of Materials (BOM) for panel hardware: brackets, cable trays, strain reliefs, grounding lugs, mounting hardware, thermal labels/warnings

## Tools

- AutoCAD / DraftSight (mechanical CAD for panel layout drawings)
- Thermal modeling software (ANSYS Fluent / FloTHERM, or simplified steady-state heat calculations in Excel)
- Electrical CAD tools (Eplan, SEE Electrical) for integrated electrical-mechanical coordination
- 3D modeling (SolidWorks, Fusion 360) for complex enclosures and interference checking
- Excel / structured markdown (thermal calculation, BOM, procedure documentation)
- NEMA/IEC standard references for enclosure ratings and thermal guidelines

## Standards

- IEC 62208:2016 — Safety of Machinery — Graphical Symbols for Operator Controls and Signalling Devices — thermal and enclosure design guidance
- IEC 61551:2021 — Safety of Machinery — Thermal Equipment — thermal dissipation and ventilation requirements
- NEMA 250 (IEC 60529 equivalent) — Enclosure Types (4, 4X, 4D, 12, 12K, 13, etc.) and environmental protection ratings
- IEC 60204-1 — Safety of Machinery — Electrical Equipment of Machines (Section 6: Thermal considerations and cooling)
- IEC 61000-6-2 / IEC 61000-6-4 — Electromagnetic Compatibility — requirements for EMC in industrial environments (informs cable separation and grounding)
- VDI 3341 (optional, German standard) — Design and implementation of electrical control panels for machinery
- ISO 11064 — Ergonomics of Human-System Interaction — control room/panel ergonomics

## IEC 62443 Lifecycle Stages

- R2 (Detailed Design — panel layout finalized with thermal validation)
- R3 (Implementation — panel assembled per approved layout and design)

## Roles

- DES (Design Engineer) — primary: creates layout drawings, performs thermal calculations, validates spatial requirements
- Mechanical Engineer — supports with enclosure selection, thermal management, and ergonomic layout
- Electrical Engineer — coordinates electrical routing (conduit, cable trays, grounding) with mechanical layout
- Manufacturing/Assembly Technician — provides input on feasibility and assembly sequence
- Safety/Compliance Engineer — verifies NEMA ratings, IP protection, and thermal safety compliance

## Dependencies

### Hard Dependencies (Required Before Execution)

- SK-D06-006: Component Selection and Procurement — cannot design panel layout without knowing component dimensions, power ratings, and mounting requirements
- SK-D06-002 ⏳: Single-Line Diagram and Control Wiring Design — cannot route cables without knowing electrical connectivity and current requirements
- Panel environmental specifications and NEMA rating requirement — cooling strategy and enclosure type depend on environmental constraints

### Soft Dependencies (Enhances Effectiveness)

- SK-D05-001 ⏳: SCADA/EMS/DERMS Functional and Software Design — functional requirements inform component selection and panel scope
- SK-D06-001 ⏳: Panel Standards and Design Rules — customer-specific panel design standards may specify layout conventions or thermal limits

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Layout drawing is complete and clear: all components are located and dimensioned, cable routing is shown, service access is unobstructed
2. Component placement rationale is documented: grouping of related components (e.g., PLC modules, terminals for a single subsystem) follows logical operational flow
3. Thermal calculation is performed per IEC 62208 methodology: heat dissipation is quantified for all components, cooling method is selected and sized appropriately
4. Worst-case thermal scenario is analyzed: maximum ambient temperature + maximum component power dissipation; calculated enclosure temperature is within safe limits (typically < 50°C for electronics, < 40°C for PLC terminals)
5. Cable separation and routing follows electromagnetic compatibility practices: power and signal cables are separated (typically 100 mm minimum), high-current conductors are isolated, grounding paths are clear
6. NEMA/IP rating is verified: enclosure sealing, ventilation openings, and component accessibility are consistent with specified protection rating
7. Panel assembly procedure is documented with clear step-by-step instructions, torque specifications for mounting bolts, and testing/acceptance criteria
8. BOM is complete and accurate, linked to approved component selections from SK-D06-006
9. Design has been reviewed and approved by DES, electrical engineer, mechanical engineer, and manufacturing/assembly representative

## Estimated Effort

| Level | Effort | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Junior (< 2 yr) | 8–12 person-days | Assumes single enclosure, ~15–25 major components, natural ventilation cooling, standard layout; includes CAD drafting, thermal calculation, and documentation |
| Senior (5+ yr) | 4–7 person-days | Same scope; senior can rapidly develop layouts from templates and perform streamlined thermal calculations |

Notes: Large multi-enclosure systems or active cooling (HVAC) may require 1.5–2× effort due to complex thermal modeling and coordination between enclosures. Projects requiring 3D interference checking and custom enclosures may add 3–5 person-days.

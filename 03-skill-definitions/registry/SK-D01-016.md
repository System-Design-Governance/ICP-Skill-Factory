## Description

Develop a comprehensive incident response procedure that defines the end-to-end workflow for identifying, classifying, responding to, and recovering from information security incidents affecting OT/ICS systems. This skill produces an operationally deployable procedure document covering incident classification criteria, severity levels, escalation paths, damage control tasks, recovery time objectives, and post-incident improvement mechanisms. The boundary of this skill is procedure design and documentation; the actual execution of incident response during a live incident falls under SK-D01-017 ⏳ (Security Incident Investigation and Forensics), and the continuous security monitoring that detects incidents falls under SK-D01-018 ⏳.

## Inputs

- Organization's information security policy framework (ISMS context)
- Asset inventory with criticality ratings (from SK-D01-005: Asset Inventory Development)
- Risk assessment findings (from SK-D01-006 / SK-D01-007) — risk scenarios inform incident classification
- Business continuity management procedures (ISMS-P-06 or equivalent — for activation decision criteria)
- Regulatory requirements for incident reporting (sector-specific: energy, critical infrastructure)
- Customer requirements for incident notification timelines and procedures
- Existing vendor support contracts and SLA details (for external collaboration provisions)

## Outputs

- Incident Response Procedure Document (structured per ID24 exemplar format):
  - Incident definition and scope (affecting confidentiality, integrity, availability of systems/services/networks)
  - Incident response workflow flowchart (threat intelligence → evaluation → notification → emergency → restoration → improvement → record keeping)
  - Incident classification system with 4-level severity matrix (factors: confidentiality impact, completeness impact, applicability, business criticality)
  - Response execution procedures by incident level
  - Escalation criteria and paths
  - Damage control task assignments (A–G: impact assessment, containment, recovery, investigation, evidence preservation, reporting, communication)
  - Post-incident procedures (recovery, investigation, improvement mechanism)
- Roles and Responsibilities Matrix: Information Security Processing Team, Emergency Response Team composition and activation criteria
- RTO Targets by Incident Level: Level 1–2 (72 hours), Level 3–4 (36 hours), or as defined by customer requirements
- External Collaboration Provisions: vendor contract references, law enforcement coordination procedures
- Incident Reporting Templates: standardized forms for incident notification, status update, and closure report
- Training Requirements: incident response team competency and drill schedule

## Automation Potential

**Level:** Partial

Incident notification workflows, escalation timer triggers, and reporting template generation can be automated through incident management platforms. Post-incident metrics collection (time-to-detect, time-to-contain, time-to-resolve) is automatable. However, incident classification judgment, damage assessment, containment strategy selection, and post-incident root cause analysis require human expertise.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Procedure covers the complete incident lifecycle: identification → classification → containment → eradication → recovery → lessons learned
2. Incident classification system defines at least 4 severity levels with explicit criteria (confidentiality, integrity, availability impact factors)
3. Each severity level has defined RTO targets and escalation paths with named responsible parties
4. Damage control tasks (A–G per ID24 exemplar) are assigned to specific roles with defined activation criteria
5. External collaboration provisions document vendor support activation procedures and law enforcement coordination protocols
6. Post-incident improvement mechanism includes a reporting timeline (e.g., 1-month post-incident review per ID24 §5.7.8) and lessons-learned integration process

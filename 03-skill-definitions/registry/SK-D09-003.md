## Description

Develop single-line diagrams representing the electrical power distribution system including switchgear, transformers, generators, loads, protection devices, and metering points. SLDs are the primary engineering reference for power system analysis (D03) and protection device engineering (D04), and serve as a critical artifact for security analysis and threat modeling of electrical infrastructure. This skill covers the translation of power system design requirements into standardized single-line diagram notation per IEC 60617 and IEEE Std 91-1984, ensuring consistency with network topology, fault analysis requirements, and operational control schemes. The boundary of this skill is the development and validation of the SLD artifact itself; the detailed electrical calculations and fault analysis fall under D03, and protection device settings engineering falls under D04.

## Inputs

- Power system architecture and network topology (from SK-D02-001 ⏳: OT Network Topology Design)
- Equipment specifications: transformers, switchgear, generators, loads, metering points (from D09.1 equipment design activities)
- Fault analysis requirements and protection philosophy (from D04 requirements; typically covers fault levels, protection coordination zones)
- Control and monitoring requirements (from SCADA/DCS system architecture in D12)
- Customer electrical specifications and standards (SOW, design codes per jurisdiction)
- Existing one-line diagrams or legacy system documentation (brownfield projects)

## Outputs

- Single-Line Diagram in CAD format (dwg, pdf) or vector format (visio, draw.io) with all major electrical components represented
- Equipment schedule/bill of materials cross-referenced to SLD (component ID, rating, settings)
- SLD legend and notation key per IEEE Std 91 or IEC 60617 standard
- Conduit/cable routing diagram or cable schedule integrated with SLD
- Protection device coordination summary (zones, fault levels, device settings cross-referenced to SLD component IDs)
- SLD narrative document describing system architecture, major electrical components, and design decisions

## Tools

- AutoCAD / Revit (primary CAD platform)
- Microsoft Visio / draw.io (secondary for preliminary design reviews)
- ETAP / PSS/E (power system simulation tools for validation of SLD against electrical models)
- Excel (equipment schedule and protection device settings tables)
- IEC 60617 and IEEE Std 91 symbol libraries (embedded in CAD tools or custom libraries)

## Standards

- IEC 60617: Graphical Symbols for Electrical and Electronics Diagrams — primary standard for SLD notation
- IEEE Std 91-1984: Graphic Symbols for Electrical and Electronics Diagrams (including supplements) — widely used in North America
- IEC 62443-3-2: Security Risk Assessment for System Design — SLDs as artifacts for threat modeling of electrical infrastructure
- ANSI C37.20.1: Switchgear and Controlgear — equipment classification and nameplate standards
- Customer electrical design codes (e.g., NERC CIP, utility-specific standards)

## IEC 62443 Lifecycle Stages

- R2 (Detailed Design — SLD finalized as reference for protection engineering and security analysis)
- R3 (Implementation — SLD drives equipment procurement and installation)

## Roles

- DES (Design Engineer) — primary: develops SLD, validates completeness and correctness against electrical requirements
- PE (Protection Engineer) — provides protection device placement and coordination requirements
- SYS (System Architect) — provides system topology and control requirements
- PLC (Programmer/Controls Engineer) — validates SCADA monitoring points mapped to SLD

## Dependencies

### Hard Dependencies (Required Before Execution)

- SK-D02-001 ⏳: OT Network Topology Design — SLD must reflect the approved network architecture and physical locations
- D09.1 equipment design — specific equipment ratings and control interface requirements feed SLD development

### Soft Dependencies (Enhances Effectiveness)

- SK-D03-001 ⏳: Fault Analysis Design (Preliminary) — fault levels inform protection zones and device placement on SLD
- D04 Protection Device Design — protection coordination philosophy guides SLD layout and device zones
- SK-D12-002 ⏳: Protocol Parser Development — SCADA monitoring points are mapped to SLD components

## Acceptance Criteria

1. SLD covers 100% of electrical components in the system architecture: transformers, switchgear, generators, loads, metering points, protection devices
2. All major electrical components are labeled with consistent nomenclature (component ID) tied to equipment schedule
3. SLD notation conforms to IEEE Std 91 or IEC 60617 standard; deviations documented with customer approval
4. All protection devices and metering points are shown on SLD with coordination zones clearly marked
5. Cable/conduit routing diagram or schedule is cross-referenced and consistent with SLD
6. SLD has been reviewed and approved by Design Engineer (DES), Protection Engineer (PE), and System Architect (SYS)

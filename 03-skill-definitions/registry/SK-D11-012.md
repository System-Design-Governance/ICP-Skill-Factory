## Description

Plan, execute, and manage the annual engineering training program that develops personnel competencies aligned with the Engineering Competency Framework (SK-D11-011), covering training needs analysis, curriculum design, delivery scheduling, effectiveness evaluation, and certification tracking. Under ICP governance (GOV-SDP), the training program is part of the annual governance cycle: competency gaps identified during KPI assessment drive the next year's training plan, creating a closed-loop development system. Security training (IEC 62443 competency) is the primary instantiation, but the program is generalized to all engineering disciplines per ADR-006. The boundary of this skill is training program management (planning, scheduling, tracking, evaluation); the competency framework that defines what competencies to develop falls under SK-D11-011, and individual project-level training material development falls under SK-D09-008 ⏳.

## Inputs

- Engineering Competency Framework (from SK-D11-011) — defines target competencies, proficiency levels, and assessment criteria per role
- Competency gap analysis results — individual engineer assessments against framework (from SK-D11-011 assessment instruments)
- GOV-SDP annual governance cycle: data freeze date, assessment window, training budget allocation
- KPI assessment results (from SK-D11-021 ⏳) — performance gaps that indicate training needs
- ID21 (Tier 3): QP-02 Personnel and Training Management Procedure — organizational training governance, forms, evaluation templates
- Available training resources: internal subject matter experts, external training providers, certification programs (IEC 62443, CISSP, vendor certifications)
- Department budget and resource allocation for training activities

## Outputs

- Annual Training Plan Document:
  - Training needs analysis: aggregated competency gaps across all roles, prioritized by business impact and gap severity
  - Training curriculum: course/module inventory mapped to competency framework items
    - Internal training: in-house courses, mentoring programs, project-based learning assignments
    - External training: vendor courses, industry conferences, certification preparation programs
    - Self-directed learning: recommended reading, online courses, lab exercises
  - Delivery schedule: quarterly training calendar with course dates, instructors, target participants, and venues
  - Budget allocation: per-course cost, total annual training budget, cost-per-person metrics
  - Success metrics: target training hours per person, certification targets, post-training assessment targets
- Training Execution Records:
  - Attendance tracking: per-course participant records with completion status
  - Effectiveness evaluation: post-training assessments (knowledge tests, practical demonstrations), participant feedback, manager evaluation of on-the-job application
  - Kirkpatrick Level 1–3 evaluation: Reaction, Learning, Behavior change metrics
- Certification Tracking Register: per-person certification status, expiry dates, renewal requirements, and recertification schedule
- Training Effectiveness Report (annual): year-over-year competency improvement metrics, gap closure rates, ROI analysis of training investment
- Annual Governance Cycle Input: training outcomes feed into next-year KPI targets and competency framework updates (closed-loop)

- R0 (Foundational — training program established as ongoing organizational capability)
- R1–R5 (Continuous — training activities occur throughout all lifecycle stages; specific training may be triggered by project-phase competency needs)

## Roles

- Head of System Design (GOV-SDP) — primary: owns training program, approves annual plan and budget, reviews effectiveness report
- Security Engineering Role (GOV-SDP) — contributes security-specific training requirements, may serve as internal trainer for IEC 62443 topics
- Design QA Role (GOV-SDP) — validates training plan alignment with competency framework gaps, reviews assessment quality
- All 7 GOV-SDP roles — participate as training recipients; provide feedback on training effectiveness and relevance
- PM (Project Manager) — coordinates training schedule with project delivery timelines (training should not conflict with critical project milestones)

## Automation Potential

**Level:** Partial

Training scheduling and calendar management can be automated (LMS integration, automated reminders). Certification expiry tracking and renewal alerts can be fully automated. Training attendance and completion tracking can be automated through LMS platforms. Competency gap-to-training-need mapping can be partially automated (rule-based recommendation from gap analysis results). However, training needs prioritization requires strategic judgment (business impact assessment), curriculum design requires instructional design expertise, training delivery effectiveness evaluation requires qualitative assessment, and annual training plan approval requires leadership review of organizational priorities.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Annual training plan produced with training needs analysis linked to competency framework gaps: every identified gap has a corresponding training intervention mapped
2. Training curriculum covers all 7 GOV-SDP roles with role-specific development paths: no role without a targeted training track
3. Delivery schedule specified with quarterly granularity: course dates, instructors, and participant lists defined for minimum Q1 and Q2 at plan approval time
4. Training effectiveness evaluated using minimum Kirkpatrick Level 2 (Learning): post-training knowledge assessment conducted for every formal training course
5. Certification tracking register maintained: all personnel certifications tracked with expiry dates; renewal actions initiated minimum 90 days before expiry
6. Annual training effectiveness report produced: year-over-year competency metrics, gap closure rates, and training ROI documented; report feeds into next annual governance cycle (per GOV-SDP)

## Estimated Effort

| Level | Effort | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Junior (< 2 yr) | 10–15 person-days | Assumes 7-role department, ~15–20 engineers; includes needs analysis, plan authoring, scheduling, and one evaluation cycle |
| Senior (5+ yr) | 5–8 person-days | Same scope; senior leverages established training templates and vendor relationships |

Notes: Annual training plan is a recurring deliverable; initial creation requires more effort (15–20 person-days including framework alignment), subsequent years require update effort only (8–12 person-days). Training delivery effort (instructor time, course development) is separate from program management effort. Certification costs vary significantly by type: IEC 62443 cybersecurity specialist certification ~$3,000–5,000 per person; vendor certifications vary. ID21 QP-02 provides the organizational procedure that the training program must comply with.

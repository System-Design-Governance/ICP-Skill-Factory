## Description

Produce fabrication-ready CAD drawings for control panel and equipment cabinet manufacturing, enabling the fabrication shop and assembly teams to construct the physical control panel structures and mechanically mount all components. Fabrication drawings include front and rear panel views (elevations), drilling patterns for component mounting holes, DIN rail layouts showing relay and drive positions, cable duct and conduit routing paths, knockouts for cable entry, nameplate and labeling specifications, material callouts, and manufacturing tolerances. These drawings must be dimensionally accurate, producible by shop personnel without engineering interpretation, and coordinated with the electrical design (schematic, wiring diagrams, terminal block schedule) to ensure that mechanical layout supports all electrical connections. Drawings are produced in standard CAD formats (AutoCAD, SolidWorks, or equivalent) and must include revision control per SK-D09-005 to track engineering changes through the fabrication cycle.

## Inputs

- Functional electrical schematic diagrams (from SK-D04-001 ⏳: Functional Electrical Schematic Design)
- Wiring diagram and terminal block schedule (from SK-D06-002: Wiring Diagram Development; SK-D06-003: Terminal Block Schedule Development)
- Equipment and component datasheets (relay modules, PLC, drives, power supplies, terminal blocks, DIN rails, cable ducts)
- Panel specification document (internal/external dimensions, material, mounting location, NEMA/IP rating requirements)
- Wire sizing results and cable schedule (from SK-D06-005: Wire Sizing Calculation; SK-D02-003 ⏳: Interface Control Document)
- Control panel layout standards and organizational design guidelines
- Manufacturing or site-specific material and fabrication constraints

## Outputs

- Fabrication drawing set (PDF and native CAD format, e.g., .dwg or .sldprt):
  - Front panel (face) elevation with component cutouts, indicator light positions, pushbutton positions, selector switch locations, and nameplate location
  - Rear panel (back) elevation with terminal block arrangement, cable duct routing, cable entry knockouts, grounding point locations, and mounting rails
  - Left/right side panels (if applicable) showing cable routing and ventilation requirements
  - Top view showing DIN rail layout, component mounting positions, and cable tray/duct routing
  - Detail views of critical features: drilling patterns with hole positions and dimensions, DIN rail mounting details, cable entry grommet locations
  - Material schedule: panel material (steel, aluminum, stainless steel), thickness, paint/coating specification, gasket materials (if environmental sealing is required)
  - Nameplate and labeling specification: font size, color, location of wire numbers, terminal block labels, equipment labels
  - Engineering change tracking and revision block (per SK-D09-005)
- Assembly instructions document (text or annotated drawing) for panel shop personnel explaining the sequence of assembly, component mounting order, and special handling notes
- Bill of Materials (BoM) for panel fabrication: structural components (frame, panels, rails), hardware (mounting brackets, fasteners), non-electrical items (cable ducts, labels, grommets)
- Coordination matrix linking fabrication drawing features to electrical design elements (e.g., "Terminal block TB-01 is located at rear panel position [coordinates]; see wiring diagram sheet 2")

## Tools

- AutoCAD or AutoCAD Electrical (primary; supports parametric DIN rail libraries and standard panel components)
- SolidWorks / CATIA (if 3D panel modeling is required for complex geometries or interference checking)
- Eplan Pro Panel (integrated EPLAN module for 2D panel layout and BoM generation; syncs with electrical design in Eplan Electric)
- Excel (component schedule, BoM, coordination matrix)

- IEC 60617: Graphical symbols for use in electrical and electronics engineering — symbol conventions for panel layout diagrams
- IEC 61439-1 / IEC 61439-2: Low-voltage switchgear and control gear assemblies — panel design, safety, and assembly standards
- IEC 61131-2: Programmable controllers, Part 2: Equipment requirements and tests — cabinet and panel mechanical and environmental specifications
- ISO 1219 (Fluid Power): Graphic symbols and circuit diagrams (if hydraulic/pneumatic systems are integrated)

## IEC 62443 Lifecycle Stages

- R2 (Detailed Design) — fabrication drawings are the primary deliverable enabling transition from design to manufacturing

## Roles

- DES (Design Engineer) — primary: produces fabrication drawings, coordinates with electrical design team, ensures correctness and completeness
- Mechanical/Fabrication Engineer — reviews drawings for manufacturability, provides feedback on assembly constraints and cost optimization
- Manufacturing/Panel Shop Lead — uses drawings for panel fabrication; may request clarifications or design modifications for constructability

## Dependencies

### Hard Dependencies (Required Before Execution)

- SK-D06-002: Wiring Diagram Development — fabrication drawings must show terminal block locations and cable routing consistent with the wiring diagram
- SK-D06-003: Terminal Block Schedule Development — terminal block schedule informs the physical arrangement and labeling on the fabrication drawings
- SK-D06-005: Wire Sizing Calculation — wire sizes and cable types inform the sizing of cable ducts and conduit specifications

### Soft Dependencies (Enhances Effectiveness)

- SK-D04-001 ⏳: Functional Electrical Schematic Design — schematic provides the electrical logic that fabrication layout must accommodate
- SK-D02-003 ⏳: Interface Control Document — inter-panel cable entry points and cable routing must be reflected in the fabrication drawing

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Fabrication drawings completely define the physical structure and component layout of the control panel with all dimensions necessary for fabrication (no "field fit and finish" ambiguities)
2. All component mounting positions (relays, PLC, drives, terminal blocks, DIN rails) are shown with coordinates or relative dimensions from a common origin
3. All drilling patterns, knockout locations, and cable entry points are dimensioned and shown with manufacturing tolerances appropriate to the material and process
4. DIN rail layout clearly shows the planned layout of relay modules, drives, and I/O modules with part numbers and mounting positions
5. Cable duct and conduit routing is shown in plan (top view) and elevations (front/rear), with bend radii and support locations specified
6. Nameplate and labeling specification includes font size, color, position, and method of attachment (engraved, printed label, embossed, etc.)
7. Material schedule lists all structural materials, paints, coatings, gaskets, and fasteners with sufficient detail for procurement
8. Assembly instructions or notes are clear enough for shop personnel to assemble the panel without design engineering interpretation
9. Fabrication drawings are 100% coordinated with electrical design: terminal block locations match wiring diagram; cable entry points match cable schedule; component positions are compatible with electrical connections
10. All drawings are revision-controlled per SK-D09-005 and have been reviewed and approved by the DES lead and Mechanical/Fabrication Engineer

Notes: Complex, custom enclosures (e.g., outdoor cabinets with environmental sealing, multi-level internal structure, or integrated heat exchangers) may require 1.5–2× effort. Revisions to electrical design require updates to fabrication drawings.

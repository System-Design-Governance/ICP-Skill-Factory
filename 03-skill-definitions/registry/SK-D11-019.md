## Description

Manage the ownership, maintenance, and exception arbitration for engineering standards used across all projects, per GOV-SDP governance framework. This skill establishes the organizational standards governance structure, assigning responsibility for standard ownership, managing standard updates and revisions, and providing an escalation-based exception arbitration process for standard deviations. The organization maintains a hierarchy of engineering standards covering design, implementation, testing, and operational disciplines (security standards are one instantiation; the framework is generalized per ADR-006). Standards ownership creates accountability: each standard has a named owner (typically a senior engineer or role) responsible for maintaining that standard, evaluating deviations, and proposing updates. Exception requests (requests to deviate from a standard for a specific project) are evaluated using a structured L1-L2-L3 escalation protocol: L1 is project-level deviation approval by the Project Manager; L2 is cross-project exception requiring Head of System Design ruling (with technical review); L3 is organizational standard change requiring engineering management approval. The boundary of this skill is standards governance and exception arbitration; the actual content of individual standards falls under their respective domain skills (SK-D01-024 for vendor security standards, SK-D08-001 for testing standards, etc.).

## Inputs

- Organizational standards register (catalog of all active standards, with ownership assignments, version history)
- Standards owner nominations / role assignments (from GOV-SDP organizational structure)
- Standards revision requests (from project teams, engineering roles, or lessons learned)
- Exception requests (project-level deviations from standard with business justification)
- Approval authorities per escalation level:
  - L1: Project Manager (for project-level scope deviations)
  - L2: Head of System Design (for cross-project or strategic exceptions)
  - L3: Engineering management / organizational leadership (for standard change decisions)
- GOV-SDP (Tier 1): Standards Governance Framework — policies and procedures for standards ownership, exception evaluation, and escalation
- Historical exception decisions and their outcomes (for precedent and consistency)

## Outputs

- Standards Register (living document): inventory of all active standards, with fields: standard ID, standard name, version, owner name/role, effective date, next review date, applicability scope (projects/roles), status (Active, Under Review, Deprecated)
- Standards Owner Assignment Matrix: ownership assignments per standard, with roles and contact information; covers all standards in register
- Exception Request Decision Record (L1/L2/L3 per request): exception request ID, standard being deviated from, deviation description, business justification, evaluation summary, decision (Approved/Denied/Approved-with-Conditions), decision authority (L1/L2/L3 level), approval signature, date, conditions (if any)
- Exception Tracking Register: all open and closed exception requests, with status, approval level, decision date, decision authority
- Standards Maintenance Schedule: timeline for standard reviews, revisions, and organizational updates (e.g., quarterly standards review cycle aligned with governance cycle)
- Standards Update/Revision Package (per standard update):
  - Revision change summary: sections changed, rationale for changes, impact assessment
  - Updated standard document (version incremented)
  - Implementation guidance (if applicable)
  - Lessons learned inputs (if update triggered by exception outcomes)
- Escalation Authority Delegation Matrix: documentation of who is authorized to approve decisions at L1, L2, L3 levels (normally: PM for L1, Head of System Design for L2, Engineering Management for L3)
- Exception Outcome Review Report (periodic, e.g., annual): aggregated data on exception trends, L1/L2/L3 distribution, patterns indicating standards that need revision, recommendations for organizational policy adjustments

- R0–R5 (Continuous): standards ownership and exception arbitration occur throughout all lifecycle stages; specific standards may apply to different stages (e.g., design standards apply to R1-R2; testing standards apply to R3-R4)

## Roles

- Head of System Design (GOV-SDP) — primary owner of this skill: oversees standards governance framework, makes L2 exception decisions, approves standard updates, maintains standards register
- Standards Owners (per standard) — named roles responsible for individual standards: evaluate change requests, ensure standard relevance, recommend revisions, support L2 exception evaluation
- PM (Project Manager) — L1 authority: approves project-level deviations from standards; submits exception requests on behalf of project; implements approved exceptions
- Project teams / Engineers — initiate standards revision requests or exception requests through PM or standards owner
- Engineering Management / Leadership — L3 authority: approves organizational standard changes (when exception indicates standard should be revised), approves standards governance policy updates
- Design QA Role (GOV-SDP) — may support standards governance: ensures standards are current, identifies standards gaps, supports standards owner duties

## Dependencies

### Hard Dependencies (Required Before Execution)

- None formally; however, standards governance effectiveness depends on the standards themselves being well-defined (in the respective domain skills: SK-D01-024 for vendor security standards, SK-D08-001 for testing standards, etc.). If standards are poorly defined or ambiguous, exception requests and arbitration become more subjective and difficult.

- SK-D01-024: Vendor Security Management and Oversight — vendor security standards are one category of standards governed by this skill

## Automation Potential

**Level:** Partial

Standards register maintenance, version tracking, and approval workflows can be automated through document management systems (version control, change tracking, workflow automation). Exception request routing and escalation can be partially automated (workflow rules: L1 requests → PM queue, L2 requests → Head of System Design queue, L3 requests → Management queue). Notification systems for standards reviews and expiring approvals can be fully automated. However, technical evaluation of exception requests requires domain expertise (standards owner and approvers must evaluate business justification and technical risk), exception approval decision-making requires human judgment, and organizational decision-making about standard changes (L3) requires leadership deliberation. Precedent matching and consistency checking can be partially automated (flagging similar past exceptions for reviewer reference).

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Standards Register is complete and current: every active organizational engineering standard is listed with ownership assignment and effective date; register is reviewed and updated minimum quarterly
2. Standards Ownership Matrix assigns a responsible role to every standard: no standard without a named owner; every owner has clearly documented authority, contact information, and review responsibilities
3. Exception Request Process is documented with L1-L2-L3 escalation criteria defined: criteria for L1 (project-level) vs. L2 (cross-project) vs. L3 (standard change) decision are explicitly stated (not subjective)
4. All exception decisions are recorded with decision authority and approval signature: every exception decision document identifies the escalation level (L1/L2/L3) and is signed by the designated authority for that level
5. Standards Update Cycle is established and followed: standards are reviewed minimum annually; revision triggers are documented (e.g., exception patterns, lessons learned, regulatory changes); all standard updates are version-controlled and tracked in standards register
6. Exception Outcome Review is conducted periodically (annually minimum): aggregated exception data is analyzed for trends; if patterns indicate a standard should be revised, formal revision request is submitted and tracked

## Estimated Effort

| Level | Effort | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Junior (< 2 yr) | 0.5–1 person-day per exception request | Evaluation and decision time; assumes straightforward requests with clear L1/L2/L3 classification |
| Senior (5+ yr) | 0.25–0.5 person-day per exception request | Same scope; senior can quickly assess precedent and make decisions |

Notes: Standards register maintenance (quarterly update) typically 1–2 person-days per quarter. Standards owner role (per standard) requires 0.5–1 person-day per month for review, revision coordination, and exception consultation. L1 (PM-level) exception decisions are typically fast (1–2 days approval time); L2 (Head of System Design) decisions may require technical review and take 3–7 days; L3 (management) decisions may take 1–3 weeks depending on organizational approval cycle. Annual standards effectiveness review and revision planning: 2–4 person-days. Organization-wide standards governance setup (initial establishment of framework, register, ownership assignments): 10–15 person-days one-time. Subsequent governance maintenance: 2–4 person-days per month (ongoing).

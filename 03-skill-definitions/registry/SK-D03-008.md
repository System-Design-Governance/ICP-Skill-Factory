## Description

Analyze harmonic distortion in electrical networks caused by power electronics devices (inverters, variable frequency drives, rectifiers) and design passive and/or active harmonic filters to meet IEEE 519 power quality standards and customer compatibility requirements. Identifies non-linear loads and harmonics sources, calculates harmonic current/voltage propagation through the network, and specifies filter types, locations, and tuning parameters. This skill informs equipment specification, zone design, and cybersecurity architecture by identifying critical power quality infrastructure that must be protected, monitored, and integrated into security control strategies. Poor harmonic performance can indicate equipment malfunction or cyber-physical attacks.

## Inputs

- Power System Model with component impedance details (from SK-D03-010: Power System Modeling)
- Equipment inventory specifying power electronics (inverters, VFDs, rectifiers, switching power supplies):
  - Device type, rating, control mode
  - Switching frequency and modulation type
  - Current injection characteristics and harmonic spectra (from manufacturer or measurement)
  - Geographic location and interconnection point
- Historical power quality measurements or site survey data:
  - Harmonic voltage/current spectra (preferably at multiple points in the network)
  - Total Harmonic Distortion (THD) at critical buses
  - Individual harmonic magnitudes (3rd, 5th, 7th, etc.)
  - Power factor measurements
- Compatibility requirements:
  - IEEE 519 or IEC 61000-2-2 limits for the site classification
  - Customer voltage/current distortion limits
  - Sensitive equipment requirements (e.g., medical devices, precision manufacturing)
- Existing harmonic mitigation (if any): capacitor banks, reactors, filters
- Network topology and planned DER/load growth scenarios

## Outputs

- Harmonic Analysis Report including:
  - Identified harmonic sources and their current injection characteristics
  - Harmonic current propagation and voltage distortion throughout the network
  - Bus-by-bus harmonic voltage and current profiles
  - Identification of resonance conditions (if present)
  - Comparison to IEEE 519 and customer compatibility limits
  - Identification of critical buses and equipment with harmonic exposure
  - Sensitivity analysis (impact of future load growth on harmonics)
- Harmonic Filter Design Specification:
  - Filter type selection (passive series/shunt, active filters, hybrid)
  - Filter location and quantity
  - Component values (L, C, R) for passive filters or control parameters for active filters
  - Tuning frequency and damping characteristics
  - Response time and bandwidth requirements
  - Harmonic impedance curves and attenuation plots
  - Integration with existing capacitor banks and reactors
- Predicted performance after filter installation (harmonic mitigation curves)
- Equipment specification for filter hardware (transformer, capacitors, inductors, active filter controller)
- Monitoring and control logic specification (if active filter or auto-tuning is required)
- Power quality monitoring plan and alert thresholds

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Harmonic analysis covers all non-linear loads and power electronics devices in the system with documented current injection spectra
2. Harmonic voltage and current distortion calculated at minimum at all network buses with sensitive equipment or high DER concentration
3. All predicted harmonic levels comply with IEEE 519 Recommended Practice (or customer-specified limits) after filter installation
4. Filter design validated with resonance analysis: no resonance peaks near power electronics switching frequencies or network impedance changes
5. Individual harmonic attenuation (at least 3rd, 5th, 7th, 11th, 13th) and filter impedance curves provided
6. Power factor impact of filter installation assessed; no capacitor over-voltage conditions under any operating scenario
7. Filter sizing validated for continuous operation and transient overload conditions
8. Harmonic analysis and filter design reviewed and approved by SYS, PRAC, and verified against field measurements (if existing site)

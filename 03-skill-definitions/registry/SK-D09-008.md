## Description

This skill encompasses the design and development of comprehensive training materials for system operators, administrators, and maintenance personnel covering system operation, security procedures, alarm handling, incident response basics, and maintenance tasks. Training materials are designed to support adult learning principles and are evaluated using the Kirkpatrick Model (L1—reaction, L2—learning, L3—behavior transfer). Unlike SK-D09-006 (Operation Manuals, which focus on procedural reference), this skill focuses on instructional design, learning objectives, content sequencing, assessment methods, and multimedia presentation. Training materials feed directly into SK-D11-012 (Engineering Training Program Management), which manages the training delivery and evaluation. The scope includes instructor guides, learner workbooks, presentations, hands-on exercises, and self-assessment tools.

## Inputs

- System functional design and operation procedures (from SK-D05-001: Control System Functional Specification and SK-D09-006: Operation Manual Writing)
- Security policies and access control procedures (from SK-D01-020: Account and Access Control)
- Alarm and event procedures (from SK-D05-010: Alarm and Event Management)
- HMI screen designs and operator interface (from SK-D05-005: HMI Screen Design)
- Cybersecurity incident response procedures (from SK-D01-024: Incident Response Planning)
- Safety procedures and SIL requirements (from SK-D01-018: Safety Instrumented System Design)
- Organizational training policies and learning management system (LMS) specifications
- Target audience analysis: operators, administrators, maintenance technicians (roles, experience levels, learning preferences)

## Outputs

- Instructor Guides: complete lesson plans with learning objectives, content outline, delivery notes, timing, and assessment methods
- Learner Workbooks: participant handouts with key concepts, practice exercises, case studies, and self-assessment questions
- Presentation Decks: slides with speaker notes, illustrations, videos, and interactive elements
- Hands-On Exercise Scenarios: realistic simulations or lab setups for practical skill development
- Assessment Tools: quizzes, practical tests, and competency checklists aligned with Kirkpatrick L2 and L3 objectives
- Training Schedule and Audience Matrix: mapping training modules to audience roles and competency requirements
- Kirkpatrick Evaluation Plan: L1 (reaction) survey, L2 (knowledge) test, and L3 (behavior) post-training observation criteria

## IEC 62443 Lifecycle Stages

- R3 (Implementation) — training materials are developed in parallel with system implementation
- R4 (Commissioning/Startup) — training is delivered to plant operators and maintenance personnel prior to system handover
- R5 (Operations) — refresher training and competency verification are ongoing per organizational training policies

## Roles

- DES (Design Engineer) or TMS (Training and Materials Specialist) — primary: leads instructional design and content development
- Subject Matter Experts (SMEs) — provide technical accuracy for system operation, security, and maintenance content
- OPS (Operations Lead) — reviews training for operational relevance and provides feedback from plant operator perspective
- STC (Security Technical Consultant) — reviews security training modules for completeness and alignment with security policies
- Trainer/Instructor — may be involved in piloting materials and providing feedback on delivery effectiveness

### Soft Dependencies (Enhances Effectiveness)

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Training materials cover all required competencies: system startup, normal operation, alarm response, security procedures, basic troubleshooting, and maintenance tasks
2. Learning objectives are clearly stated for each training module, written in measurable behavioral terms (e.g., "Learner will correctly respond to a high-pressure alarm within 30 seconds")
3. Content is organized with clear prerequisite sequencing; advanced topics build on foundational concepts
4. Assessment methods (quizzes, practical exercises, competency checklists) are aligned with L2 and L3 Kirkpatrick evaluation objectives
5. Instructor guides include delivery notes, timing guidance, answer keys, and facilitation tips for engaging learners
6. Training materials have been reviewed by both Subject Matter Experts (technical accuracy) and intended audience representatives (usability and relevance)

## Description

Identify, classify, and map all stakeholders involved in the OT/ICS cybersecurity project, analyzing their interests, influence levels, communication preferences, and decision authority to enable effective engagement throughout the project lifecycle. Stakeholder analysis produces the stakeholder register and communication plan that ensure the right people are informed, consulted, and empowered at each project stage. This skill covers stakeholder identification and analysis; the actual stakeholder engagement execution (meetings, presentations) is embedded in each project activity skill, and the formal Gate 0 stakeholder review falls under SK-D14-015.

## Inputs

- Customer/employer organizational structure and contact information
- Project scope and requirements (from SK-D14-001)
- Tender documents — evaluation panel, decision-makers, technical contacts
- ICP project team structure and GOV-SDP role assignments
- Prior engagement history with the customer (if available)

## Outputs

- Stakeholder Register: per-stakeholder entry with: name, organization, role, interest area, influence level (High/Medium/Low), attitude (Supportive/Neutral/Resistant), communication preference, decision authority scope
- Stakeholder Influence/Interest Matrix: 2×2 classification (High/Low influence × High/Low interest) for engagement strategy
- Communication Plan: per-stakeholder-group communication frequency, channel, content type, and responsible ICP contact
- Interface Register: organizational interfaces requiring formal communication protocols (ICP ↔ customer, ICP ↔ subcontractor, customer ↔ regulator)

## Roles

- Pre-Gate Design Support Role (GOV-SDP) — primary: conducts stakeholder analysis, produces register and communication plan
- PM (Project Manager) — manages ongoing stakeholder relationships, uses communication plan for engagement
- Head of System Design (GOV-SDP) — reviews stakeholder landscape for governance implications

## Dependencies

### Hard Dependencies (Required Before Execution)

- None — stakeholder analysis can begin as soon as project opportunity is identified

### Soft Dependencies (Enhances Effectiveness)

- SK-D14-001: Requirements Specification Development — project scope clarifies which stakeholder groups are relevant
- SK-D14-017: NDA and Confidentiality Agreement Management — NDA status affects what can be discussed with which stakeholders

## Automation Potential

**Level:** Partial

Stakeholder register template generation and CRM integration can be automated. Communication plan template generation from stakeholder classification can be automated. However, stakeholder identification requires organizational research and relationship knowledge, influence/interest assessment requires political judgment, and communication strategy requires understanding of cultural and organizational dynamics.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Stakeholder register covers all major stakeholder groups: customer decision-makers, technical contacts, subcontractors, regulators, and ICP internal team — no key groups omitted
2. Every stakeholder has influence/interest classification with engagement strategy assigned (Manage Closely / Keep Satisfied / Keep Informed / Monitor)
3. Communication plan specifies frequency, channel, and content type per stakeholder group — actionable and specific
4. Interface register identifies all formal organizational interfaces requiring communication protocols
5. Stakeholder analysis reviewed by PM and Pre-Gate Design Support Role — validated against known organizational landscape

## Estimated Effort

| Level | Effort | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Junior (< 2 yr) | 2–3 person-days | Assumes medium-complexity project with 10–20 stakeholders; includes identification, analysis, and documentation |
| Senior (5+ yr) | 1–2 person-days | Same scope; senior leverages organizational knowledge and rapid stakeholder classification |

Notes: Effort increases with organizational complexity (multi-department customers, international stakeholders, regulatory involvement). Large government projects with complex approval chains may require 2× effort. Stakeholder analysis is a living document — initial effort is front-loaded but requires periodic updates throughout the project.

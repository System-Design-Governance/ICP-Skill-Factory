## Description

Assess and assign Security Level Targets (SL-T) to each zone/conduit in the architecture per IEC 62443-3-3. SL-T defines the required security capability and control depth for each zone. This assessment feeds SK-D01-001 (Zone/Conduit Architecture Design) and SK-D14-014 (Preliminary Security Classification).

Per GOV-SD SL Decision Lifecycle, SL-T decisions follow a formal progression: initial assignment → verified by stakeholders → updated based on risk assessment → finalized in SL-T baseline.

**Scope Boundary:** SL-T assignment methodology and assessment only. Detailed control specification for each SL-T level falls under SK-D14-014 (Preliminary Security Classification); actual control implementation falls under later phases.

**Adjacent Skill References:**
- SK-D01-001: Zone/Conduit Architecture Design (produces architecture for SL-T assignment)
- SK-D01-006: Preliminary Threat and Risk Assessment (provides risk input to SL-T decisions)
- SK-D14-014: Preliminary Security Classification (receives SL-T baseline)

## Inputs

- Zone/Conduit architecture diagram (from SK-D01-001 or contemporaneous)
- Risk assessment findings (from SK-D01-006 or earlier risk analysis)
- IEC 62443-3-3 requirements and guidance
- Criticality and consequence analysis for each zone
- SL Decision Lifecycle process documentation
- Stakeholder risk tolerance and business impact constraints

## Outputs

- SL-T Baseline Document
  - SL-T assignment for each zone (SL-T 0 through SL-T 4)
  - Justification for each SL-T assignment (linked to risk and consequence)
  - SL-T matrix mapping zones to target levels
  - Verification records (stakeholder sign-offs at each lifecycle stage)
  - SL Decision Lifecycle audit trail (initial → verified → updated → final)
  - Transition rules for escalated/de-escalated SL-T decisions

## Roles

- Security Architect (SAC) — Lead assessor and SL-T assignment authority
- Risk Manager — Risk and consequence input to SL-T decisions
- Zone Owner/Stakeholder — Zone criticality and impact assessment input
- Compliance Officer — Regulatory and governance input to SL-T targets
- Verification Lead — SL Decision Lifecycle stage verification and sign-off

## Dependencies

### Hard Dependencies

- Zone/Conduit architecture must be defined (or at minimum, preliminary architecture available)
- Risk assessment findings (from SK-D01-006 or equivalent) must be available
- Stakeholder consensus on business criticality and acceptable risk levels
- IEC 62443-3-3 training and competency of SAC

### Soft Dependencies

- Historical SL-T data from similar systems (informative reference)
- Industry-specific SL-T guidance (e.g., for power systems, chemical processes)
- Regulatory SL-T requirements (may constrain SL-T targets)

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Each zone in the architecture has an assigned SL-T (0 through 4) documented in the SL-T Baseline.
2. Each SL-T assignment is justified by explicit reference to risk level, consequence severity, or regulatory requirement.
3. Justifications clearly link zone criticality, potential impact (safety, business, operational), and resulting SL-T level.
4. The SL Decision Lifecycle is documented with evidence of progression through initial → verified → updated → final stages.
5. Stakeholder sign-offs are collected and recorded at the verified and final stages of the SL Decision Lifecycle.
6. Any SL-T escalation or de-escalation decisions are documented with rationale and approvals.
7. The SL-T Baseline is approved in writing by the Security Architect and at least one Zone Owner/Stakeholder representative.
8. The SL-T assignment aligns with IEC 62443-3-3 target selection guidance (e.g., no arbitrary assignment).

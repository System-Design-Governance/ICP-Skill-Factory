## Description
Execute integrated risk assessment combining IEC 62443-based cyber risk analysis, FMEA (Failure Mode and Effects Analysis), and HAZOP (Hazard and Operability Study) per GOV-SD mandatory triple-method requirement at Tier 1 governance level. This integrated approach produces unified risk findings with explicit cross-method traceability: threats identified via STRIDE/DREAD (SK-D01-008) are marked T-XXX, FMEA failure modes are marked FM-XXX, and HAZOP deviations are marked HAZ-XXX. The integrated assessment delivers comprehensive risk characterization that addresses cyber threats (IEC), system failures (FMEA), and process hazards (HAZOP) in a single coordinated deliverable. Gate 3 (post-design review) verification requires ≥20% sampling of integrated findings with documented corroboration across methods.

## Description — Objectives
- Execute three complementary risk assessment methods (IEC 62443 cyber risk, FMEA, HAZOP) in a coordinated, integrated workflow
- Identify threats, failure modes, and hazards with explicit method-specific identifiers (T-XXX, FM-XXX, HAZ-XXX)
- Create integrated risk register mapping findings across methods to show overlaps, gaps, and reinforcing controls
- Assign unified risk scores that account for cyber, failure, and hazard perspectives
- Establish Gate 3 verification sampling (≥20%) with documented corroboration evidence across assessment methods
- Generate a unified integrated risk assessment report suitable for executive governance review

## Description — Scope
**In Scope:** Execution of three assessment methods (IEC 62443 cyber risk, FMEA, HAZOP) within a single integrated workflow; cross-method traceability and mapping; unified risk scoring; integrated risk register creation; Gate 3 verification sampling with corroboration evidence; governance reporting.

**Out of Scope:** Individual method development (STRIDE/DREAD methodology under SK-D01-008; FMEA/HAZOP methodology covered separately in industry standards); control design or implementation (handled by D01/D02 implementation skills); operational risk monitoring or continuous assessment; incident-specific root cause analysis (handled separately).

## Description — Key Concepts
- **Integrated Risk Assessment:** Single coordinated assessment process that combines cyber risk (threats/vulnerabilities), system failure risk (failure modes/effects), and process safety hazards (operability deviations) in unified analysis
- **Cross-Method Traceability:** Explicit linkage between findings from different assessment methods, showing whether threats, failure modes, and hazards address overlapping risk areas or complementary risks
- **Method-Specific Identifier:** Unique identifier tracking finding origin: T-XXX (IEC threat), FM-XXX (FMEA failure mode), HAZ-XXX (HAZOP deviation)
- **Unified Risk Score:** Composite risk quantification considering cyber exploitability, failure likelihood, and hazard severity in a single framework
- **Gate 3 Verification:** Post-design review checkpoint requiring ≥20% sampling of integrated risk findings with documented evidence of corroboration across ≥2 assessment methods
- **Corroboration Evidence:** Documentation showing that a finding identified in one method is confirmed, refined, or clarified by another method

## Description — Methodology
Integrated risk assessment follows a structured eight-phase process:

**Phase 1: Scope and Method Coordination Setup**
- Define assessment boundary: full system, subsystems, specific process areas?
- Establish scope alignment across three methods (same architectural scope, same criticality classification)
- Assign assessment team with expertise in all three methods: cyber security (STRIDE/DREAD), reliability engineering (FMEA), and process safety (HAZOP)
- Create integrated assessment schedule coordinating the three workflows
- Establish cross-method review and traceability processes

**Phase 2: IEC 62443 Cyber Risk Assessment**
- Execute STRIDE/DREAD threat modeling (SK-D01-008) to enumerate cyber threats
- For each threat identified, assign T-XXX identifier (e.g., T-001, T-002, etc.)
- Document threat details: threat type (STRIDE category), attack vector, prerequisites, impact scenario
- Score threat severity using DREAD dimensions (Damage, Reproducibility, Exploitability, Affected Users, Discoverability)
- Identify applicable IEC 62443-3-3 functional requirements that address each threat
- Create cyber threat catalog with T-XXX identifiers and IEC requirement linkage

**Phase 3: FMEA (Failure Mode and Effects Analysis)**
- Identify critical system components and functions (from architecture and process flow)
- For each component/function, enumerate potential failure modes (what can go wrong?)
- Assess effects: what is the consequence of each failure mode on system operation, safety, availability?
- Estimate failure likelihood (L: 1=rare to 3=frequent)
- Assess impact severity (S: 1=minor to 3=catastrophic)
- Identify current controls or mitigating factors (detection capability D: 1=certain to detect, 3=unable to detect)
- Calculate Risk Priority Number (RPN) = L × S × D for each failure mode
- Assign FM-XXX identifiers (e.g., FM-001, FM-002) to high-priority failure modes
- Document recommended corrective actions for high-RPN items

**Phase 4: HAZOP (Hazard and Operability Study)**
- Apply HAZOP guidewords (NO, MORE, LESS, AS WELL AS, PART OF, REVERSE, WRONG, etc.) to process parameters
- For each guideword application, identify potential deviations from intended operation (what shouldn't happen but could?)
- Assess consequences: hazardous outcome, safety risk, operational impact
- Identify root causes: design flaw, procedural failure, component failure, external event
- Assign HAZ-XXX identifiers (e.g., HAZ-001, HAZ-002) to identified hazards
- Evaluate existing safeguards and risk reduction measures
- Recommend additional controls or risk reduction (if residual risk is unacceptable)

**Phase 5: Cross-Method Mapping and Traceability**
- Create integrated risk register mapping findings across methods:
  - Cyber threats (T-XXX) that may cause or contribute to failure modes (FM-XXX) or hazards (HAZ-XXX)
  - Failure modes (FM-XXX) that create cyber vulnerabilities or trigger process hazards
  - Hazards (HAZ-XXX) that may be triggered by cyber incidents or system failures
- Identify overlapping risks: where two or more methods identify related risks (corroboration)
- Identify unique risks: where only one method identifies a specific risk (complementary coverage)
- Document cross-method dependencies and reinforcements

**Phase 6: Unified Risk Scoring**
- For each integrated risk entry, establish unified risk score considering:
  - Cyber exploitability (from DREAD or STRIDE analysis)
  - Failure likelihood and severity (from FMEA RPN)
  - Hazard likelihood and consequence (from HAZOP assessment)
- Develop unified risk matrix or scoring algorithm that accommodates all three risk dimensions
- Classify unified risks as High, Medium, Low based on aggregate scoring
- Prioritize integrated risks for control design and mitigation

**Phase 7: Gate 3 Verification Sampling**
- Sample ≥20% of integrated risk findings (minimum 10 items if <50 total findings)
- For each sampled finding, document corroboration evidence:
  - **Type 1 Corroboration:** Same or related finding identified by ≥2 methods with aligned severity/priority
  - **Type 2 Corroboration:** Finding from one method validated or refined by investigation in another method
  - **Type 3 Corroboration:** Expert review confirming that finding addresses real, credible risk
- Create verification matrix showing sampled findings with corroboration type and evidence
- Document any discrepancies or method conflicts (e.g., HAZOP identifies high risk, FMEA estimates low likelihood)
- Prepare Gate 3 verification report for governance review

**Phase 8: Governance Reporting and Risk Classification**
- Prepare Integrated Risk Assessment Report suitable for executive governance review
- Include executive summary: high-level findings across all three methods, unified risk landscape
- Present integrated risk register with T-XXX, FM-XXX, HAZ-XXX findings, unified risk scores, and recommended controls
- Report Gate 3 verification sampling results and corroboration evidence
- Provide residual risk assessment post-control implementation
- Identify any method conflicts or open questions requiring governance decision

## Description — Practical Guidance
- **Pitfall:** Treating three methods as separate, sequential activities without integration. **Guidance:** Conduct methods concurrently or in rapid succession; cross-reference findings throughout (not just at the end); establish coordinated assessment team.
- **Pitfall:** Identifying findings from all three methods but no cross-method traceability or corroboration. **Guidance:** Create integrated risk register explicitly showing T-XXX ↔ FM-XXX ↔ HAZ-XXX mappings; identify overlapping vs. complementary risks.
- **Pitfall:** Insufficient Gate 3 verification sampling or vague corroboration evidence. **Guidance:** Sample ≥20% of findings; for each sample, document Type 1/2/3 corroboration with specific evidence (e.g., "threat T-015 causes failure mode FM-007 per FMEA analysis; both rated High priority").
- **Pitfall:** Unified risk scoring that ignores one method's perspective (e.g., high cyber risk but low failure/hazard score). **Guidance:** Develop scoring algorithm that accommodates all three dimensions; document method conflicts and resolve with expert rationale.
- **Pitfall:** Cyber risk disproportionately weighted over process safety or reliability. **Guidance:** Ensure HAZOP and FMEA findings receive equal governance attention; unified score should reflect balanced consideration of all three risk types.
- **Pitfall:** Assessment results not connected to control design or implementation. **Guidance:** Each high-risk finding must map to specific controls (from D01/D02 skills); integrated assessment should drive control prioritization and selection.

## Description

Develop the technical risk matrix that identifies, classifies, and prioritizes technical risks that could impact the successful delivery of the OT/ICS cybersecurity project, providing the risk pre-disclosure input required for Gate 0 decision-making. This skill covers project-level technical risks (delivery risks, integration risks, technology risks) that are distinct from the security-specific threat and risk assessment (SK-D01-006/SK-D01-007). Under GOV-SD, the risk pre-disclosure list is one of the 5 mandatory Gate 0 inputs. The boundary of this skill is pre-project technical risk identification and prioritization; the formal cybersecurity risk assessment falls under SK-D01-006/SK-D01-007, and the cost risk contingency analysis falls under SK-D14-016 ⏳.

## Inputs

- Requirements specification (from SK-D14-001) — scope defines risk exposure areas
- Feasibility assessment (from SK-D14-003) — technical risks identified during feasibility
- Site survey findings (from SK-D14-011) — constraint-related risks
- Existing infrastructure inventory (from SK-D14-012) — integration and compatibility risks
- Preliminary security classification (from SK-D14-014) — security ambition-related risks
- ICP historical project risk data: common risk patterns from similar projects

## Outputs

- Technical Risk Matrix:
  - Per risk: risk ID (TR-nnn), risk description, risk category (Technology / Integration / Delivery / Resource / External), likelihood (High/Medium/Low), impact (High/Medium/Low), risk score (L × I), mitigation strategy, risk owner, residual risk after mitigation
  - Risk categories:
    - Technology risks: vendor product maturity, technology obsolescence, compatibility issues
    - Integration risks: legacy system interfaces, protocol compatibility, multi-vendor coordination
    - Delivery risks: timeline pressure, scope creep, resource availability
    - Resource risks: skill gaps, key person dependency, subcontractor performance
    - External risks: regulatory changes, customer organizational changes, supply chain delays
  - Risk priority ranking: sorted by risk score for decision-maker focus
- Risk Pre-Disclosure List (Gate 0 input): top-N risks formatted for executive review, with business impact description and recommended actions
- Risk Mitigation Action Plan: per high-priority risk, specific actions with owner, deadline, and success criteria

## Automation Potential

**Level:** Partial

Risk register template generation and scoring calculation can be automated. Historical risk pattern matching (similar project risk lookup) can be partially automated. Risk priority ranking and heat map generation can be automated. However, risk identification requires contextual analysis, likelihood and impact assessment require expert judgment, and mitigation strategy design requires creative problem-solving.

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Technical risk matrix covers all five risk categories (Technology, Integration, Delivery, Resource, External) — no category empty without documented rationale
2. Every risk has: unique ID, description, category, likelihood, impact, risk score, mitigation strategy, and risk owner — no risks with incomplete records
3. Risk pre-disclosure list produced with top risks in business-understandable language — suitable for Gate 0 decision-makers (per GOV-SD)
4. High-priority risks (risk score ≥ threshold) have specific mitigation action plans with owner, deadline, and success criteria
5. Risk matrix reviewed by SAC, SYS, and PM — multi-perspective risk validation completed

## Estimated Effort

| Level | Effort | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Junior (< 2 yr) | 3–5 person-days | Assumes medium-complexity project; includes risk identification workshop, matrix development, and pre-disclosure list authoring |
| Senior (5+ yr) | 2–3 person-days | Same scope; senior leverages historical risk patterns and rapid assessment |

Notes: Risk identification benefits from multi-disciplinary workshop format (SAC + SYS + PM together). Effort increases with project novelty — first-of-a-kind projects have more uncertain risks. Risk matrix is a living document updated throughout the project.

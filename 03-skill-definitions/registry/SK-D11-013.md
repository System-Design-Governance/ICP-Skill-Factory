# Skill Definition — SK-D11-013

> **Skill**: Information Asset Classification and Grading applies organizational data classification policy to all information assets, establishing explicit protection requirements that inform security control design, NDA scope, and handling procedures. Classification is the prerequisite for risk-driven security decisions across D01 and D14.

## Description

Classify and grade all information assets (documents, data files, configurations, credentials, test results, audit records) according to organizational data classification policy (ID23 Tier 3 procedure) and ISO 27001 Annex A requirements, assigning explicit confidentiality and integrity protection levels that inform security control design and implementation. This skill establishes the classification framework and applies it consistently to the solution scope, creating the asset registry that feeds downstream decision-making in D01 (security control selection), D14 (documentation handling), and D11 (access control policy). The scope includes: defining asset types and classification criteria, assessing each asset's confidentiality/integrity/availability impact, assigning classification grades (e.g., Public, Internal, Confidential, Restricted), documenting handling requirements per grade, and maintaining a classified asset inventory. The actual implementation of security controls that protect classified assets falls under domain D01; this skill defines what assets require protection and at what level. Soft dependency on SK-D01-033 ⏳ (Data Classification Policy) for higher-level policy framework; this skill executes the operational classification process.

## Inputs

- Data Classification Policy ID23 (organizational standard for asset grades, protection criteria, handling rules)
- Asset inventory with data type/sensitivity assessment (from SK-D01-005 or equivalent, listing information assets in scope)
- Preliminary risk assessment findings (asset criticality, threat landscape from SK-D01-006 ⏳)
- Regulatory/contractual data protection requirements (GDPR, NDA terms, customer DPA, industry standards)
- Information flow and system architecture diagram (understanding which assets move where)
- Existing classification precedents or legacy grading schemes (for consistency and transition planning)
- Access control requirements and stakeholder roles (from SK-D11-001: Governance Structure)

## Outputs

- **Information Asset Classification Matrix**: listing of all information assets with assigned grades (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability levels), handling rules, and storage location/medium
- **Asset Classification Register**: structured inventory (Excel/Airtable) organized by asset type, grade, owner, custodian, retention period, and audit trail
- **Classification Criteria Mapping Document**: rationale for each asset's grade, traceable to ID23 policy, risk assessment, and regulatory drivers
- **Data Handling and Protection Requirements Table**: per-grade specifications for:
  - Access authorization (who may view/modify)
  - Encryption requirements (at rest and in transit)
  - Physical security and facility controls
  - Retention and disposal requirements
  - NDA and confidentiality agreement scope
  - Audit logging and monitoring requirements
- **Classification Training and Acknowledgment Records**: stakeholder training on asset classification scheme, signed acknowledgments of classification responsibilities
- **Classification Governance Schedule**: frequency of re-assessment (triggered by new assets, project phase transitions, or annual review), owner responsibilities, and sign-off process

## Tools

- Excel / Airtable (asset classification matrix and register, filtering by grade, owner, location)
- Confluence / SharePoint (classification policy publication, training materials, acknowledgment tracking)
- Data Discovery Tools (e.g., Talend, Collibra, or manual inventory for smaller scopes) — optional for larger asset bases
- Document Management System (for storing classified assets with embedded metadata tags)
- Policy Management Platform (for versioning and distribution of classification criteria)

## Standards

- ISO 27001:2022 Annex A.5.1.2: Information Classification and Handling — requirements for classifying information and assigning protection levels
- IEC 62443-2-1 §7.1.2: Information Management — procedures for protecting information assets according to classification
- IEC 62443-3-2 §7.1.3: Information and System Assets — identification and classification of information assets as a foundational control
- NIST SP 800-122: Guide to Protecting the Confidentiality of Personally Identifiable Information — supplementary guidance for PII classification (if applicable)

## IEC 62443 Lifecycle Stages

- R0 (Concept) — establish asset classification policy framework and initial asset list
- R1 (Security Requirements) — classify information assets from security requirements definition, assign SL-T per asset type
- R4 (Security Validation & Verification) — verify that all test data, audit records, and validation artifacts are classified and protected appropriately
- Additional review at R5 (Operations) for new operational data and configuration baselines

## Roles

- **Information Asset Owner (SOP/Process Owner)** — responsible for the data; determines initial classification grade
- **Data Custodian / System Administrator** — implements handling requirements, maintains asset register, enforces access controls
- **Classification Authority (GOV-SDP)** — reviews and approves classifications, ensures consistency with ID23 policy, handles re-classification requests
- **Compliance / Legal** — advises on regulatory (GDPR, contractual NDA) implications for classification levels
- **Security Architect (SAC)** — uses classifications to inform security control requirements and risk assessment

## Dependencies

### Hard Dependencies (Required Before Execution)

- ID23: Data Classification Policy (Tier 3 procedure) — organizational standard for asset grades and protection criteria; this skill operationalizes ID23
- SK-D01-005 or Asset Inventory — must know what information assets exist before classifying them
- SK-D11-001: Organizational Governance Structure — roles and authorities for classification approval

### Soft Dependencies (Enhances Effectiveness)

- SK-D01-033 ⏳: Data Classification Policy (higher-level policy definition, if separate from ID23)
- SK-D01-006 ⏳: Threat and Risk Assessment — risk findings inform criticality/grade assignments
- SK-D14-017: NDA Development and Management — NDA scope and confidentiality terms align with classified asset handling requirements
- SK-D01-024: Vendor Security Risk Assessment — vendor access to classified assets must align with vendor classification level and controls

## Acceptance Criteria

1. 100% of identified information assets in scope have a documented classification grade (Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability) traceable to ID23 policy
2. Every classified asset has documented handling requirements specifying: access authorization rules, encryption mandate, physical storage, retention period, and destruction method
3. Asset Classification Register is complete and current within 5 business days of any new asset identification or lifecycle transition
4. Classification rationale document provides explicit traceability from each asset's grade to ID23 policy criteria and risk assessment findings (no subjective justification such as "appropriate level")
5. 100% of information asset owners and custodians have received classification training with documented acknowledgment
6. At least one internal audit confirms that 95% of sampled assets are handled per their assigned classification requirements
7. All NDA and vendor access agreements reference or comply with classified asset handling requirements

## Estimated Effort

| Level | Effort | Notes |
|-------|--------|-------|
| Junior (< 2 yr) | 5–8 person-days | Assumes ~100 distinct information assets; requires SME input for criticality assessment |
| Senior (5+ yr) | 2–4 person-days | Leverages classification templates and rapid asset type recognition |

Notes: Classification of 500+ assets or complex multi-stakeholder data flows may require 1.5–2× effort and external data discovery tools. Annual re-assessments typically require 0.5–1 person-day; incremental new asset classification averages 1–2 hours per asset type.

## Description

Develop, maintain, and govern Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for engineering activities across all IEC 62443 project lifecycle stages (R0 through R5). This skill covers SOP authoring methodology, review cycle governance, version control, distribution and training, and compliance monitoring. SOPs provide the procedural foundation that all other skills (D01–D14) operate within, ensuring that security engineering decisions are documented, repeatable, and auditable. The scope includes initial SOP development, periodic review and update cycles triggered by process changes or audit findings, version control and change history, stakeholder sign-off procedures, and post-publication monitoring of adherence. This skill does not execute the technical security activities themselves—those fall within domain D01–D14—but rather ensures that all activities occur within a governed procedural framework. Note: SK-D11-004 (SOP Development under D09.3) was merged into this centralized skill during R5 normalization to eliminate duplication and establish a single authoritative SOP governance responsibility.

## Inputs

- Project Charter and Security Policy framework (from project initialization, per ID01 §6.5.1.2)
- Stakeholder roles and responsibilities matrix (RACI, from SK-D11-001)
- Existing SOP templates and organizational process standards
- Regulatory, contractual, and compliance requirements (from Scope Definition, ID01 §6.5.1.1)
- Technical skill requirements and work instructions from SMEs across D01–D14 domains
- Process change requests from project teams, audit findings, or management reviews
- Version control repository and change log baseline (if inheriting legacy SOPs)

## Outputs

- **SOP Master Document Suite**: complete set of engineering SOPs organized by domain (D01–D14) and lifecycle stage (R0–R5), each in template format with:
  - Purpose, scope, applicability (which roles, which lifecycle stages)
  - Prerequisites and entry criteria
  - Step-by-step process flow with decision points
  - Roles and responsibilities (who performs, who reviews/approves)
  - Quality acceptance criteria (observable, measurable)
  - Cross-references to standards, tools, and related SOPs
  - Example templates or checklists where applicable
- **SOP Version Control Log**: document ID, author, version number, effective date, change description, approval sign-offs, superseded version reference
- **SOP Distribution and Training Record**: list of stakeholders trained, training date, acknowledgment of receipt, refresher schedule
- **Compliance Monitoring Checklist**: sampling plan for audit reviews of SOP adherence, metrics (% of activities per SOP, exceptions logged), reporting frequency
- **Change Management Register**: tracking of SOP modification requests, impact assessment, approval workflow, and implementation timeline
- **SOP Index and Quick Reference Guide**: organized listing of all active SOPs with keywords, owner contact, and lifecycle applicability

## Standards

- IEC 62443-2-1: Security Management System — establishes requirements for documented procedures and process control
- IEC 62443-3-2 §7.1.1: Specification of Security Requirements — procedures and controls required for security engineering
- ISO 9001:2015 §4.4: Determination of Scope, §8.1 Operational Planning and Control — organizational process documentation and control
- PRAC: Industry standard practice for IEC 62443 governance SOP structures and lifecycle management

## IEC 62443 Lifecycle Stages

- R0 (Concept) — establish SOP requirements and governance framework
- R1 (Security Requirements) — develop SOPs for requirements definition and validation activities
- R2 (Detailed Design) — develop SOPs for design, verification, and review processes
- R3 (Implementation) — develop SOPs for development, integration, and deployment execution
- R4 (Security Validation & Verification) — develop SOPs for testing, inspection, validation activities
- R5 (Operational Maintenance) — develop SOPs for operations, monitoring, incident response, change management

## Roles

- **SOP Owner (GOV-SDP)** — primary: manages overall SOP portfolio, prioritizes development, monitors compliance
- **SOP Author** — drafts procedures, incorporates SME input, maintains consistency with organizational standards
- **Domain SME** — contributes technical expertise, reviews procedure steps, validates against field practice
- **Review Authority** — formal review of SOP for clarity, completeness, compliance with standards (SAC, STC, or Quality role)
- **Training Coordinator** — organizes stakeholder training, maintains training records, schedules refreshers
- **Compliance Monitor** — conducts periodic audits, logs adherence issues, reports trends

## Dependencies

### Hard Dependencies (Required Before Execution)

- SK-D11-001: Organizational Governance Structure — must have defined roles, responsibilities, and authorities before establishing SOPs
- Project Charter and Scope Definition (ID01 §6.5.1) — SOP scope and applicability are determined from project security requirements

### Soft Dependencies (Enhances Effectiveness)

- SK-D11-017: Security Policy Development — organizational security policy provides the strategic framework that SOPs operationalize
- SK-D14-001: Documentation and Change Management — versioning and change control practices complement SOP governance
- All D01–D14 skills — inputs from domain experts during SOP authoring ensure technical accuracy and feasibility

## Acceptance Criteria

1. SOP Master Suite covers all mandatory activities for at least two consecutive lifecycle stages (e.g., R0–R1 or R2–R3), with documented rationale for phased scope
2. Every SOP includes explicit entry criteria, step-by-step process, decision rules, and observable acceptance criteria (no subjective language such as "adequate" or "appropriate")
3. Every SOP specifies roles with RACI accountability (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed) and review/approval authority
4. Version Control Log is current within 5 business days of any SOP change; all versions are archived and retrievable
5. 100% of in-scope stakeholders have completed SOP training with documented acknowledgment and completion dates
6. Compliance monitoring records for the past 90 days exist, showing at least one sampling audit per major SOP category with pass/exception tracking
7. All SOPs are cross-referenced to applicable standards sections (IEC 62443, project-specific requirements, organizational policies) and to related SOPs

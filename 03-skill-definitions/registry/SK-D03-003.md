## Description

Assess voltage stability margins of power systems under various loading conditions and contingency scenarios. Identifies voltage collapse risks and determines reactive power compensation requirements necessary to maintain stable operation. Uses time-domain and steady-state simulation tools (ETAP, PSS/E) to analyze voltage response at critical system buses under normal and stressed conditions. This skill informs zone-level power quality and availability requirements and feeds into the security architecture design by identifying critical voltage support infrastructure that must be protected and prioritized in control strategies.

## Inputs

- Power System Model (from SK-D03-010: Power System Modeling)
- Load flow analysis results (from SK-D03-001: Power Flow Analysis)
- Short-circuit study results (from SK-D03-002: Short Circuit Analysis)
- System contingency list (N-1, N-2 scenarios)
- Operating point scenarios (light load, peak load, seasonal variations)
- Voltage stability criteria and limits (customer-specified or per IEEE 1015)
- Reactive power resource inventory (capacitor banks, synchronous condensers, STATCOM, inverter capability)

## Outputs

- Voltage Stability Assessment Report detailing:
  - V-Q sensitivity analysis at critical buses
  - Voltage collapse point estimation under contingency scenarios
  - Reactive power margin assessment
  - Voltage stability margin curves and stability boundaries
  - Bus voltage profiles under normal and contingency conditions
  - Identification of weak buses and voltage-sensitive zones
- Reactive Power Compensation Specification:
  - Location and size of reactive power devices (capacitor banks, filters, STATCOM)
  - Voltage control setpoints and droop characteristics
  - Contingency response requirements
- Updated system model with reactive power compensation parameters
- Recommendations for voltage support infrastructure and control logic placement

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Voltage stability assessment covers all critical system buses and 100% of defined contingency scenarios (N-1 minimum)
2. Voltage collapse margin determined with documented methodology (e.g., VLmin, reactive power margin curves)
3. All buses maintain voltage within specified limits (typically ±10% of nominal) under normal and contingency conditions
4. Reactive power compensation scheme specified with explicit device types, locations, sizing, and control logic
5. Voltage stability findings traced to system architecture decisions (e.g., zone topology, asset protection hierarchy)
6. Assessment validated against field measurements or historical disturbance records where available
7. Report includes recommendations for voltage monitoring and control setpoints

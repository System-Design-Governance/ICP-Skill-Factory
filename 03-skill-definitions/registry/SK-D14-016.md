## Description

Cost Risk Contingency Analysis evaluates cost exposure and calculates risk-adjusted contingency reserves for the Component Bill of Materials (CBOM) and project budget. This skill bridges the CBOM development (SK-D14-005 ⏳) and risk matrix assessment (SK-D14-004 ⏳), ensuring that cost estimates include appropriate buffers for identified cost risks and market uncertainty. During Pre-Gate 0, contingency analysis supports realistic budget baselines and risk-informed feasibility decisions, reducing the likelihood of cost overruns in Gate 1 and beyond.

## Inputs

- Component Bill of Materials (CBOM) with unit costs from SK-D14-005 ⏳
- Risk matrix and cost risk assessments from SK-D14-004 ⏳
- Labor hours and staffing estimates from SK-D14-006 ⏳
- Historical cost variance data from similar projects (if available)
- Market volatility and supply chain risk factors
- Currency exchange rates (for multi-region deployments)
- Contingency policy and risk tolerance guidance

## Outputs

- Cost Risk Assessment Report identifying cost drivers and exposure areas
- Contingency Reserve Calculation showing base cost, risk factors, and contingency percentage
- Risk-Adjusted Project Budget with low/baseline/high scenarios
- Contingency Allocation by risk category (supply chain, labor escalation, scope change, technical)
- Cost Risk Mitigation strategies and triggers for contingency draw-down
- Cost Baseline and Budget Constraint documentation for Gate 0 approval
- Cost sensitivity analysis showing impact of key risk materializations

## Tools

- Cost estimation spreadsheets (Excel with Monte Carlo add-in or specialized software)
- Risk probability/impact matrix tools
- Historical cost variance databases
- Parametric estimation tools (e.g., proportional scaling by system complexity)
- Budget forecasting and variance tracking software
- Market pricing databases (e.g., component suppliers, labor indices)

## Acceptance Criteria

1. Cost Risk Assessment Report identifies at minimum 5 distinct cost risk drivers with probability and impact
2. Contingency Reserve Calculation shows base cost, risk adjustments, and contingency percentage (typically 10–25% for Pre-Gate 0)
3. Risk-Adjusted Budget includes three scenarios: low (base – minimal contingency), baseline (recommended), and high (worst-case)
4. Contingency allocation by category (supply chain, labor, scope, technical) sums to total contingency reserve
5. Cost sensitivity analysis quantifies impact of at minimum 3 key risk materializations (e.g., 15% parts cost escalation, 20% labor overrun)
6. Cost Risk Mitigation strategies are documented with specific triggers for contingency draw-down
7. Cost Baseline and contingency reserve approved by Finance and Project Leadership per governance authority levels
8. Contingency reserve is formally separated in budget documentation (not hidden in cost estimates) for tracking and variance management
